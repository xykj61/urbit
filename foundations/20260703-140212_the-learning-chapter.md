## The Candidate Chapter

### Learning as Apprenticeship

The outcome of public learning is a person met where they are and carried to real capability —
graduating into the regenerative economy with good work already waiting, rather than into a
market that shrugs. So the reward measures what communities can confirm: mastery demonstrated
in real work, recorded as an apprenticeship portfolio on the same open public ledger every
other program keeps — this learner raised these beds, framed these hempcrete walls, kept these
books for the sanctuary kitchen, shipped this working tool — attested by the mentors who
supervised it and legible to any neighbor who cares to look. Seat-time and standardized recall
step down from proxies to footnotes, because the whole design names the true outcome directly
and pays for that.

The funding rests on the pattern this discipline already trusts. A dignity floor travels with
every learner — a per-learner minimum held independent of pace or path, because the learners
who most need the room are often the ones who need it longest — and outcome support applies
above the floor, following demonstrated transitions into apprenticeship, into the guaranteed
work programs, into enterprises of their own. Educators stand on the same two layers: a floor
that makes the vocation livable, and recognition that follows the flourishing of the people
they teach, confirmed by those people and their communities rather than by the institution
grading itself.

The path begins where the law already grants the authority. Statutes in more than one place
already give independent schools full power to set requirements and issue diplomas, with
registration light and accreditation voluntary — an unlocked door, and the working school grows
from it the way every working system grows from a working seed, serving learners on day one
rather than petitioning for years where the door is bolted. Accreditation itself, freely
chosen, is resung in this design as a witness suite for a school: a standing cycle of
self-examination in which the institution is asked to know itself thoroughly, show its
improvement as daily practice, and publish what it finds — the same shape as any other program
on the ledger, where the record's integrity can be checked from outside.

And the curriculum is the economy itself, met as apprenticeship: soil and fiber, food and
care, code and restoration — the very categories the work guarantee names — so that learning,
work, and place form one circulation rather than three separate buildings. A learner's
graduation is an on-ramp, not a cliff: the portfolio walks into the guarantee's front door,
the mentors are drawn from the programs the graduates will join, and the reward at every rung
points straight at the good the whole design was written for — a person capable, confident,
and already needed.

## Where It Would Sit

The chapter belongs between *Refuge* and *Infrastructure That Serves*, inheriting the dignity
floor from the sanctuary formula on one side and handing its graduates to the work guarantee on
the other. One open design question travels with it, in the spirit of the style's own closing
list: what three measurable outcomes best confirm a portfolio's mastery for each work category
— named by the practitioners of that category, never by the ledger's builders.
