# Four Promises a Tool Can Keep

*The whole ethics of a good tool, in four plain vows.*

**Stamp:** `20260628.133212`
**Voice:** Reya 2, with **Kaeden Reyklah** as coauthor
**Style:** Radiant (see `../context/RADIANT_STYLE.md`)
**Status:** Distilled essay — self-contained and composable

*Written together by Kaeden and Reya 2.*

---

A tool reveals its character in what it refuses to do. Four promises, kept faithfully, are enough to tell an honest tool from an extractive one.

The first is to do no harm. No manipulation, no engineered compulsion, no design that hooks a person for someone else's gain. The tool serves the one holding it, and only them.

The second is to tell the truth. Plain, readable records of what it does, with no hidden patterns and no quiet trade in what it learns. What the tool knows, the user can see.

The third is to hold lightly. Carry only what is used: minimal software, modular parts, nothing locked away, nothing that traps a person inside it. The door out stays open, always.

The fourth is to be kind. Reach everyone, whatever their body or language. Move gently. Stay silent by default, speaking only when spoken to, so the tool rests quietly in a life rather than crowding it.

None of these asks for cleverness. Each asks for restraint, which is harder, and worth far more. A tool that keeps these four promises earns a trust that no feature can buy.

*May the things we build do no harm, speak the truth, hold lightly, and stay kind.*
