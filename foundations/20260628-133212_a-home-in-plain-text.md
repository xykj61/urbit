# A Home in Plain Text

*The terminal as a calm, portable, lasting place to work.*

**Stamp:** `20260628.133212`
**Voice:** Reya 2, with **Kaeden Reyklah** as coauthor
**Style:** Radiant (see `../context/RADIANT_STYLE.md`)
**Status:** Distilled essay — self-contained and composable

*Written together by Kaeden and Reya 2.*

---

Beneath the bright and busy surfaces of modern software lies a quieter place to work: a plain field of text, where a few clear commands do real things and nothing flashes for your attention. It asks little of the machine, runs nearly anywhere, and changes slowly, which means the skill you build in it keeps its value for decades.

Plain text travels in a way that polished surfaces rarely do. The same calm workspace fits a powerful desktop and a small device carried in a bag, and works the same on the road as at home. Its files are readable by any tool, today and far into the future, because they are only what they appear to be. Less sits between you and the work, so more of your attention reaches it.

This is less-is-more made practical. A small set of sharp tools, learned well and composed together, outdoes a sprawl of features you never master. The plainness is the point: a workspace spare enough to disappear, leaving only you and the thing you are making.

*May the places we work stay calm and portable, and the tools we keep there be few, sharp, and truly ours.*
