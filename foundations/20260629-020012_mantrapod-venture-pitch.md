Built to be the most ecological computer a person can carry, and to hand attention back rather than take it.*

## Our Mission

The Mantrapod exists to put a calm, durable, sovereign computer into a person's hands — one that protects their attention, keeps nothing it should not keep, opens for repair instead of aging into waste, and connects them straight to a local economy that returns more than it takes. It is the hardware where the whole vision becomes something you can hold.

---

## I. The Pitch

The Mantrapod is an open-hardware pocket computer with a reflective, paper-like screen, no radios of any kind, and memory that forgets by design. It carries no cellular connection and no wireless of any kind; it speaks only through a single wire, and only when you choose to connect it, and rests perfectly happy offline. Its working memory holds your session while the power is on and lets it dissolve when you set it down, so the device keeps nothing in the dark. It runs Rye OS on an open processor, puts plain Unicode text first while handling gentle multimedia with ease, and opens with a small screwdriver so its battery, its memory, and its screen can each be replaced in minutes. It holds your own root key, settles in the local currency on a shared ledger, and joins you directly to the community economy that keeps wealth where it was made. We build the device the way the rest of the work is built: simple, lovable, complete, and made to last for years.

---

## II. The Problem and the Opportunity

The pocket computer most people carry today is built on a set of quiet extractions. It is designed to hold attention rather than return it, gathering hours through engineered compulsion. It harvests the data of everyone who touches it and trades that data out of sight. It glows with blue light long into the night, against the body's own rhythm. It seals shut with glue, so a cracked screen or a tired battery condemns the whole device. It ages out of support in a year or two by design. And every app store and subscription drains a little more wealth away to distant headquarters, far from the person and the place that earned it.

The opportunity is the plain inversion of each of these. A device can be built to give attention back, to hold nothing it should not, to rest the eyes rather than strain them, to open instead of locking shut, to last for years instead of seasons, and to keep value circulating close to home. The market for such a device is every person who has grown tired of being the product — the readers, the writers, the makers, the parents who want a calmer tool for their children, the communities building something of their own. The Mantrapod is built on the opposite promises, and the promises are the product.

## III. The Device

**A reflective, paper-like screen.** The Mantrapod's display is a paper-like page that reads by the light already in the room. In daylight it needs no power for light at all, reflecting the sun the way a printed page does, perfectly clear outdoors where glowing screens wash out. When the room goes dark, a warm amber glow rises to meet it — blue-light-free, free of flicker, gentle on the eyes and kind to sleep. It refreshes fast enough for gentle motion, so it shows crisp text and quiet multimedia alike, while drawing a fraction of the power a bright glowing slab demands. Plain text comes first, rendered beautifully; images, simple motion, and music follow, each in the form the page serves best.

**Memory that forgets.** The Mantrapod holds your work in working memory while the power is on, and lets it dissolve when you set the device down. It carries no onboard store for your work waiting in the dark to be mined, which means it can leak nothing it never kept, and it asks a fresh, clear intention of you each time you begin. What you wish to keep, you keep on purpose: you commit it, deliberately, either to lasting paper through a printed page, or across the single wire to your own lasting store, where Rye OS keeps a whole and faithful history in Mantra. The ephemeral and the permanent each take their proper place, with one chosen act between them.

**Wired, or offline.** The Mantrapod has no cellular radio and no wireless of any kind. It cannot be tracked across a day or reached against your will, because it is not listening. It speaks only through a single wire — the sealed link Rye OS carries as Comlink — and only when you connect it, to your own lasting store, to a trusted machine, or to the community ledger. The rest of the time it is simply yours, offline and quiet, a tool rather than a tether.

**Rye OS on an open processor.** The Mantrapod runs Rye OS on a low-power, fully documented open processor, built on the same open instruction set the operating system already targets, so the whole system can be read all the way down to the metal. You meet it first as Rishi, the calm shell — a field of plain text, a few sharp commands, type and run and version and recall — and the bounded garden of memory keeps every cost visible and within reach of understanding. The language beneath it is written so the right thing is the easy thing and whole classes of error simply cannot be made. Unicode plain text is the first citizen; the quieter media are welcome guests. The correctness is built into the shape of the work, which is the kind of correctness a tool you depend on deserves.

**Made to open, made to mend.** The Mantrapod is screwed together, never glued. Lift the back cover with a small driver and the battery comes free on a pull-tab in a couple of minutes, the working memory lifts out on a keyed, screw-locked module in about five, and the screen releases from its cushioned bed in a quarter hour. The memory is a replaceable module that can move between devices and live a second life. The battery is a safe, long-cycle chemistry that holds its breath for years and recycles cleanly when its time comes. Inside the back cover, a small card carries codes that link to a full service guide for every part and name its recycling path, and a material ledger maps every material to where it can go next. The right to repair is not a promise printed on a box here; it is a law written into the shape of the device.

**The key in your hand, the community at the other end.** The Mantrapod holds your own root key — the single seed from which your whole presence derives, one half guarded and never leaving the device, one half shared so anyone can confirm your word. Through that key, and across the single wire, the device joins you directly to the community economy: you settle in the local currency on the shared ledger, your transactions signed and recorded in a history anyone can check. The Mantrapod is where a person's sovereignty becomes physical — identity, attention, and the means to trade, all held in one hand that opens.

## IV. What It Is Made Of

The Mantrapod is assembled from parts chosen by their qualities rather than their labels, and a sound, current option exists for every one of them today.

The costliest part, near a third of the build, is the reflective paper-like display with its blue-light-free amber light. The compute is a low-power open processor on a documented mainboard, modest in cost and generous in openness. The working memory is a compression-attached, screw-locked module — the repairable memory standard the wider industry has lately converged on, which draws far less idle power than the soldered kind and lifts out for reuse. There is no mass store for your work, by design — the device keeps only what it must to be itself and to be yours: the operating system in modest read-only flash, and your root key in a small secure element. Your working data finds no place to rest and be mined; it lives in working memory and dissolves with the power. The battery is a safe, long-life cell on a tool-free sled. The body is recyclable metal and a bio-based composite, sealed against splashes by compression gaskets and a breathing membrane rather than any toxic potting, with a floating inner cradle so a drop loads the frame and spares the screen. A quiet, short-throw keyboard with replaceable keycaps and a few gentle buttons complete it.

Early figures put the parts on the order of three hundred fifty to five hundred fifty dollars at a small batch, with a target price that begins near the cost of today's closed paper-like tablets and falls as batches grow. The price buys a device built to last a decade rather than a year, which is the only comparison that finally matters.

## V. The Business Model

The Mantrapod sustains itself through honest hardware sold at a fair margin, paired with the open-core model the rest of our work already keeps. Every design — the schematics, the enclosure, the service guides, the material ledger — is published freely and openly, so the device can be studied, improved, and rebuilt by anyone. The margin lives in the well-made physical thing and in the care around it: the assembled devices, the spare parts, the repair and refurbishment service, and the trust that comes from being the team that designed it. A memory module or a battery sold as a replacement is revenue that also keeps a device out of the waste stream, so the incentive and the ethic point the same way. Modules returned and refurbished re-enter the line with a second life and a clear grade. And the Mantrapod is the doorway into the larger ecosystem — Rye OS and the community economy — so each device deepens the commons it belongs to rather than locking a person into a store.

---

## VI. The Ecological Case

The Mantrapod is designed to be the most ecological computer a person can carry, and the ecology is a consequence of the design rather than a label added at the end. A reflective screen that reads by the light in the room draws a fraction of the usual power, which means a smaller battery, which means less material in the hand and less drawn from the earth. Memory that forgets needs no storage chip and suffers no wear, so there is simply less inside. No radios means less power still, and less to source and to fail. A body that opens with a screwdriver, on socketed and replaceable parts, lives for a decade through small repairs rather than dying whole in a year, which is the single largest saving any device can offer. Recyclable metal and bio-based composites, a material ledger that names every recycling path, and a take-back path for every part keep the loop closed. And what you choose to keep, you keep on lasting paper or in your own store, so the device hoards nothing and forgets gently. Less, made to last, made to open, made to return: that is the whole ecological argument, built into the object.

## VII. How It Joins the Rest

The Mantrapod is the hardware where the convictions become a thing you can hold. The device that forgets, named once as a distant horizon, is this device. The key you hold lives in its hands. The home in plain text is its first screen. The four promises a tool can keep — do no harm, tell the truth, hold lightly, be kind — are kept here in metal and silicon: it takes nothing from the person it serves, it keeps records anyone can read, it opens every door and locks none, and it stays quiet and gentle by default. And the money that stays close to home flows through it, so a person's own currency settles on the shared ledger straight from their own hand. The Mantrapod is the meeting place of Rye OS and the community economy — the operating system's calm craft and the marketplace's circulating value, joined in one pocket-sized, openable whole. We hold it as the next lap to grow when its time has come, and we keep it in clear view because it shows, plainly, what all the rest is ultimately for.

## The Ask

The seed round funds the first small batch from end to end: the engineering and the open schematics, the tooling for the openable enclosure, the sourcing of the reflective display and the open processor mainboard, the replaceable memory and the long-life battery, and the full library of service guides and the material ledger that make the device repairable from the first unit shipped. The timeline runs from funding to a first complete, lovable, repairable batch in the hands of real people, with revenue from the devices themselves, from parts and repair and refurbishment, and from the doorway each device opens into the wider ecosystem. The return is a computer that hands attention back, holds nothing it should not, opens instead of locking, lasts a decade instead of a season, and keeps a community's value circulating close to home — and a venture whose margin and whose conscience, at last, point the very same way.
