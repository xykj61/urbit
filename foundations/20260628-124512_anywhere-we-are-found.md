## The Heart

We build to make a joyful life ordinary. The old word for it is *eudaemonia* — the deep flourishing of a life well lived. The warmer words are loving devotion and grace: *prema* and *bhakti*, the heart turned fully toward what it loves. Everything here grows from one wish — that more people, in more places, come to live with that kind of joy.

Two commitments hold the whole of it together. The first is a love of clean, spare design: an architecture stripped to its essentials, correct and clear all the way down. The second is love in the plainest sense: compassion, regeneration, and care for every living thing. Elegant simplicity and open-hearted purpose, kept as one. That is the thesis beneath everything that follows.

## What We Build

At the center is a nonprofit that builds and keeps a free, open-source operating system, and gives it away. Around it grow open designs for the computers themselves, open blueprints for the buildings and the shared infrastructure, and enterprise plans that harmonize with nature rather than mine it. A public benefit corporation carries the part of the work that earns its keep, so the giving stays free and the building stays funded.

The work reaches into the land. Extractive farms turn, over time, into plant-based permaculture food forests — orchards in layers that build soil while they feed people, beautiful to walk through and productive for generations. It reaches into how we live: walkable communities raised from regional materials, with clean and healthy homes, trees that shade and feed the street, and everything you need within a short walk. It reaches into the tools of daily life: community-owned, repairable technology, broadband offered as shared infrastructure, and neighborhood places where a device is mended rather than discarded. And it nurtures the things that make a life worth living — art, music, collectives of movement, and the slow, companionable work of philosophy and soul.

## The Tools

Picture a place where a creator's story — their fable — is told and proven honestly, and where access to what they make is granted by a cryptographic pass that opens the door while revealing only what you choose to share. That pass lives on a phone built from open hardware, a true alternative to the two ecosystems that hold the world's pockets today, with its chip sourced and made close to home. Behind it runs a secure data layer, publicly designed and openly maintained, so the social tools built on it run faster than today's apps, stay secure by default, and carry no advertising you never asked for.

The same foundation offers free tools for making music, for making fashion, for keeping notes, and for holding a social graph that is truly yours, with room to set everything to your own liking. Beneath all of it sits a new language, written so that the right thing is the easy thing and whole classes of error simply cannot be made — the kind of correctness that public infrastructure deserves and earns.

## The Flow of Value

Value moves on a decentralized ledger that reaches agreement by committing a directed acyclic graph and reading the graph's own shape, with a stable settlement token, USDsui, as its steady unit. Public money, in this view, points toward public good. A government that issues its own currency is held back less by accounting than by what is real — the workers, the materials, the capacity already at hand — so when people stand ready and good work waits to be done, that work can be funded and done.

From that foundation comes a simple promise: anyone who wants to do work that matters can find it, and be supported with a full living, fair pay with care for health and family woven in. The work is the kind communities genuinely need — tending the food forests, raising the walkable homes, keeping the shared technology, teaching, healing, and restoring the land. Alongside it flows a state-issued currency that keeps value circulating close to home — the fuller treatment of that currency idea lives in [`20260629-014512_money-that-stays-close-to-home.md`](20260629-014512_money-that-stays-close-to-home.md). Toggle the proofs you choose to share, and a tradeable currency comes back to you, redeemable for regenerative housing, for vegan food grown in step with the land, and for nearly any honest good you can picture. The supply chains behind the earth materials, and the energy gathered to run the machines, are made verifiable and fair, so the whole of it can be trusted by anyone who looks. And the gatherings follow — the raves, the festivals, the quiet rooms of prayer — opened in ways the old world never quite allowed.

## The Help

On any path you take, you will have help. The old question is the right one — does this path have a heart? — and a path with heart makes for a joyful journey and makes the traveler strong. Anywhere you wish to be, a steady companion helps you chart the course.

Even from the lowest floor — on the tired days, the aching days, the days that begin with a long list and a sore throat — the way forward holds a hand. There are dignified places to land when life grows hard: simple rooms with good light and solid walls, a garden that feeds you while you tend it, skills to gather, and an open path onward when you are ready. No one here is used and discarded. No one is tossed about, or chosen like a bargain, or worn down by the pull of the crowd. Whoever you are, wherever you begin — working families and elders, the young and the newly arrived, makers of every kind — you are met, and kept, and found. That is the promise beneath all the rest: anywhere we go, we are found.

## The Future Is Present

None of this waits on a distant horizon. Ride through a community a few years on, and the view has already changed: trees lining every street, food forests rising where tired fields once stood, a neighborhood hub where a young person writes open code while an elder learns to call family in their first language, a paid maker reviving a laptop that would once have been waste. The pieces are already in our hands — the open code, the open designs, the willing communities, and technology grown mature enough to keep its promises. The future is present, and real. We are simply learning, together, to live inside it.
