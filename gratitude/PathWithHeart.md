## The Path With Heart

Don Juan's central counsel returns, again and again, to a single test. Look closely and deliberately at every path, he says, and try it as many times as you feel you must. Then ask the one question worth asking of any path: does it have a heart? A path with heart makes for a joyful journey — you become one with it, and it makes you strong. A path without heart weakens you, and in time it turns against you. The freedom in this teaching is wide: any path may be left, without offense to yourself or to anyone, when your heart tells you the time has come, provided the leaving stays clear of both fear and ambition.

## Personal Power

Everything we do and everything we are, in this teaching, rests on personal power. With enough of it, a single word can change the course of a life; without it, even the most magnificent wisdom slides off and changes nothing at all. Don Juan's counsel is to trust your own power above every other thing — to place your trust in yourself rather than in any teacher. He adds a bracing reminder of how much lies within reach: a person can cut anything from their life, at any moment, the instant they truly choose to.

## Impeccability

The impeccable, Don Juan teaches, need no one to guide them. By saving their energy rather than spending it on drama and self-importance, they can do for themselves everything the most accomplished seers do; all they require is a small opening and the awareness to recognize it. Impeccability here is less a moral perfection than an economy of energy — acting cleanly, wasting nothing, keeping power gathered for what matters.

## The Warrior's Single Choice

We choose only once, Don Juan says: to live as a warrior, or to live as an ordinary person. No second choice waits further down the road. The teaching is clarifying rather than grim. A choice made once, and lived from fully, frees a person from the endless small surrenders of a life never quite decided.

## Shields, Chosen Deliberately

A warrior selects, with great care, the things that make up their world. Each chosen item is a shield against the forces the warrior means to face — a deliberate set of things that bring peace and pleasure and steady the mind in moments of fright. The items chosen this way are themselves the marks of a path with heart.

## Seeing, Intent, and Awareness

The Don Juan books also carry an elaborate vision of perception itself. In brief: a person is pictured as a being of luminous energy, and perception is assembled at a single bright point that can move — and when it moves, it lights up other worlds, as real in their way as the everyday one. Behind all perceiving stands intent, the pervasive force that makes us aware. The aim of the warrior's discipline is a state of total awareness, the full reach of what a human being can perceive. We take from this its center rather than its cosmology: attention is movable and trainable, intention shapes what we are able to see, and clarity is something a disciplined life can grow toward.

## Neither Denial nor Indulgence

Denying yourself, Don Juan warns, is an indulgence of the worst kind — it lets us believe we are doing something great while it only fixes us more firmly within ourselves. The warrior's way is cleaner than either harshness or self-pampering: spend effort where it truly serves, and leave the rest alone.

## The Flight Into the Unknown

The warrior is counseled to keep no possessions on which to fix their power, and to place it instead on the true flight into the unknown. Power held lightly stays free to move; power tied to things we must defend grows heavy. The journey itself, traveled to its full length, is the worthwhile thing.

## How We Carry It

This warrior-philosophy keeps good company with the rest of what we hold. Its path with heart guides which work we take up and which we release. Its economy of energy meets our own discipline of bounding what we use and carrying no debt. Its impeccability is our correctness built into the shape of the work, so little is wasted on chasing faults. We are grateful for the framing, and we carry it forward the only way that truly honors a teaching — by living it in how we build.
