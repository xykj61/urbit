## The Sentence We Mean

A **claim** is a **bounded proposition** — something that could be false, stated narrowly enough that a person or a program could agree on what would count as proof.

*"Parity is 152 on this tree."* That is a claim. *"The system is good"* names no boundary, no failure mode, no proof — a witness would have to rewrite it before metal could answer.

In this project, claims are the **currency of the checkable room**. A witness proves **one claim** (or a small conjunction explicitly named as one stop) — one lap, one seam, one invariant — rather than a module, a vision, or a whole season. When the witness runs green, the bench may repeat the claim in plain confidence. When it runs red, the claim waits until the work earns green.

## Claim, Fact, Assertion, Vision — Four Words Kept Apart

| Word | What it is | Room |
|------|------------|------|
| **Claim** | A proposition awaiting or bearing proof | checkable when witness-bound |
| **Fact** | A signed event on the append-only log — canonical, immutable, Kumara-backed | substrate; verified by fold + signature |
| **Assertion** | An invariant stated in code (`assert`, `comptime assert`) | compile-time or debug-time checkable; feeds witnesses, does not replace them |
| **Vision** | An orienting image before proof exists | vision room until a claim crosses |

A transaction **fact** in `.bron` is not yet a bench **claim** until someone states what should hold — *"this signature verifies over these bytes"* — and a witness binds it. Caravan's `max_restarts: u32 = 10` is an assertion that shapes the design; the **claim** is *"restart count matches planned failures after three child exits"* — and `caravan/seed` selftest binds that.

Collapsing these words collapses the rooms. Vision becomes fact without a log line; assertion becomes product complete without a lap; a single green witness becomes proof of the whole roadmap.

## The Shape of a Good Claim

A claim fit for the bench has five properties:

1. **Falsifiable** — something could go wrong; green means something.
2. **Bounded** — scope is named; out-of-scope is named too.
3. **Singular** — one lap, one seam, one invariant — not the whole product.
4. **Metal-named** — which binary, which fixture, which host seam if any.
5. **Register-honest** — status line says whether it is checkable yet or still vision.

**Good claim (SLC-L1):** *"Given `tools/fixtures/slcl1_fact.bron` and deterministic seed `0x42`, sign → hex log line → parse → verify → fold balance 100; tamper one byte → verify rejects."*

**Bad claim (overbroad):** *"Linengrow receipts work."*

**Good claim (Edit 5):** *"At most one functional spec carries designed-not-built flight in HEAD."*

**Bad claim (mood):** *"We are disciplined about specs."*

SLC's **complete** discipline is claim discipline in product voice: *do the narrow thing wholly, and claim only what the work can already make true* ([`SIMPLE_LOVABLE_COMPLETE.md`](../context/SIMPLE_LOVABLE_COMPLETE.md)).

## Sizes of Claims — Lap, Seam, Invariant, Pin

Claims stack by size; witnesses should match the size they bind.

| Size | Example claim | Typical witness |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Invariant** | `mem.replace` postconditions hold on pristine std | one `rye/tests/mem_replace_test.rye` |
| **Seam** | Rishi's `:history` questions match Mantra store answers | RW-1 mirrored pair |
| **Lap** | SLC-2a Lap 2 redraws when buffer signature changes | `slc2a_ring2_redraw.rish` |
| **Suite pin** | Full regression + product witnesses green at **152** | `parity.rish` (many claims, one pin) |
| **Bench pin** | Tip `71e84be`, Cursor metal, date | RTAC row — meta-claim about the run |

**Parity arithmetic** is a meta-claim: *"the suite contains N witness blocks and all green."* Each block underneath is its own claim. Raising **152** does not retroactively prove SLC-L2; it proves **152 blocks passed**.

## One Witness, One Claim — the Law That Prevents Inflation

The most expensive mistake is letting one green run prove more than it names.

| Witness green | Binds | Does **not** bind |
|---------------|-------|-------------------|
| `crossing_manifest_seed.rish` | parse `<mark> <sha3-256-hex> <name>` | Amphora zip law, module home, sealed crossing |
| `slcl1_receipt.rish` | sign-append-fold-verify spine | receipt `.bron` shape, golden file, Open Asks |
| `amber_first_ring.rish` | one tree digest vs openssl golden | full cold season, scrub batch, sandbox key ritual |
| `slc2a_ring1_arbitrary.rish` | viewer draws arbitrary value headless | Ring 3 metalsmoke, keyboard, sovereign backend |
| `designed_not_built_witness.rish` | spec count ≤ 1 | Amber's functional truth on disk |

Headers like **PROPOSED SEED** and scope-note **out of scope** sections exist to **split** claims before witnesses attach. When a filename sounds larger than the witness, the smaller claim wins.

## Checkable Claims and Their Limits

A claim is **checkable** when a witness (or gate scan) can bind it on named metal — see [`20260704-024800_the-checkable-room.md`](20260704-024800_the-checkable-room.md).

**Narrow checkability** is still checkability. *"Ring 3 metalsmoke passes on GNOME Wayland host"* is a claim narrower than *"Pond runs everywhere."* The bench pin must carry the narrowness or the claim inflates silently.

**Partial checkability** is honest. SLC-L1 claims the spine; scope note lists boxes still open. Prose may say *"spine proven; golden receipt remains horizon"* — two claims, two registers, no collapse.

**Uncheckable** carries no shame. *"Pollinators measure the health of an invisible commons"* may orient foundations for years before any witness exists. The claim lives in the vision room until someone decomposes a testable spark and parks it on RTAC.

## How Claims Cross from Vision to Checkable

The doorway from [`TWO_ROOMS.md`](../context/TWO_ROOMS.md) is a **claim lifecycle**:

1. **Image** — vision names direction (*sealed delivery over Comlink*).
2. **Decomposition** — scope note lists in/out (*SLC-L2: one receipt, identity to identity, localhost first*).
3. **Claim statement** — one falsifiable sentence per witness stop.
4. **Witness** — Rishi script + hosted proof.
5. **Pin** — parity + TASKS + session log.

Skipping step 3 — jumping from image to parity — is how visions borrow bench authority. The discipline is to write the claim **before** wiring the witness, so red runs fail the claim, not a vague disappointment.

## Other Senses of Claim — Named, Not Confused

The same English word appears elsewhere; we keep the senses visible:

| Phrase | Sense | Room |
|--------|-------|------|
| **Witness proves a claim** | bounded proposition on metal | this essay |
| **Name claim on the log** | signed fact binding a memorable string to a key | Comlink / Linengrow design ([`20260702-014112_comlink-beyond-dns-and-sockets.md`](20260702-014112_comlink-beyond-dns-and-sockets.md)) |
| **Prove a claim without revealing data** | ZK credential — SLC-L4 horizon | vision until lap exists |
| **Plot name-claim in Realidream** | spatial fact on graph | design-only in TASKS |
| **Real-resource claim** (inflation debate) | economic assertion in survey prose | research for understanding, sourced figures |
| **Brick makes no claim about neighbors** | Brix isolation — no dependency assertion | TAME / structural |

When a document uses *claim*, the reader should ask: **bench proposition, log fact, or horizon image?** This essay governs the first.

## Writing Claims in Prose — May and May Not

**May (claim stated, witness named):**

- *"Claim: tampered resin fails verify. Witness: `tools/amber_first_ring.rish`."*
- *"Claim: redraw when signature changes. Green: `slc2a_ring2_redraw.rish` on Cursor metal."*

**May not (claim smuggled without binding):**

- *"Amber is done."* — season larger than ring 1 witness.
- *"We have a unified surface."* — Realidream vision; Skate is a smaller claim.
- *"Parity green, therefore production-ready."* — meta-claim ≠ product claim.

Session logs and specs earn trust when they **quote the claim** the commit bound, not the mood the commit celebrated.

## Claims and the Grain

A siloed pattern in the clean room **leans on grain strands** — each sentence cuts with a named commitment ([`the-grain-and-the-crossing.md`](../active-designing/20260702-184312_the-grain-and-the-crossing.md)). That is claim-writing for design prose: *"supervision readiness is a signed fact Caravan folds"* is a claim template awaiting a witness, not yet a green run.

Sameness-as-compression ([`foundations/20260703-182612_sameness-is-the-macro.md`](../foundations/20260703-182612_sameness-is-the-macro.md)) is a **principle**, not a claim — it orients how we shrink the system. *"One value model across Brix, wire, and shell literals"* is closer to a claim; individual witnesses bind pieces of it over time.
