## The Sentence We Mean

A claim lives in the **checkable room** when the project can answer it with a **witness** — a program that runs on metal, returns green or red, and binds exactly the claim it names. The bench records the result, pins the parity arithmetic when the claim joins the suite, and speaks in plain confidence because the machine answered.

The checkable room runs on **proof the machine returns**, not on belief or mood. We ran the check; metal answered green or red. That is the whole register.

Orientation, policy hope, hardware fit at horizon scale, the image of a unified surface — these may live beside the bench and feed it. They enter as fact only when they earn the same doorway every other checkable claim crosses.

## Witness — the Room's Instrument

After gate-unify ([`20260704-020800`](../context/specs/20260704-020800_one-word-one-gate.md)), **witness** names the machine proof exclusively. A witness:

1. **Names one claim** — one lap, one seam, one invariant; the whole vision waits its own witnesses.
2. **Runs red then green** — failure carries information; green is earned.
3. **Lives in Rishi** — `tools/*.rish` beside the hosted `.rye` it exercises.
4. **Registers in the trio** — `parity.rish` when the claim is suite-worthy; `parity-selftest.rish` for std symlink truth; `additive-gate.rish` when `rye/lib/` changes.

The **gate** in itinerary prose is Kaeden's precondition — doors, nods, rulings — rather than the old machine-lint sense. The checkable room **consumes** witnesses and keeps them distinct from gates. A stop waits behind a gate until Kaeden clears it; once cleared, witnesses prove the stop.

**Toll** names the price of shared order — facts that touch the commons pay it. In the checkable room, the toll shows up as extra ceremony: mirrored pairs at seams, width scans on hosted corpus, chrono sweeps when versions accrete. The center stays fast because the toll is paid at the narrows, rather than in every inner loop.

## The Trio — Repository Law in the Checkable Room

The witness trio is the checkable room's **standing constitution**:

| Member | Binds |
|--------|--------|

| `parity-selftest.rish` | `rye/lib/std` symlinks point at pristine vendor std |

Calling these "gates" in historical docs is readable; forward prose prefers **the trio** or **witness trio** so **gate** stays Kaeden's itinerary word.

Structural scans — `width-check.rish`, `tame-check.rish`, opening-lines hosts, `designed_not_built_witness.rish` — are checkable **without** parity blocks; they share the same law: red or green, no middle voice.

## Happy Zone and Thin Edge — Two Altitudes Inside the Room

The checkable room holds two altitudes. The happy-zone teaching names them:

**Happy zone** — the fold core, headless replays, pure arithmetic, stubbed seams. Checks are exact, fast, isolated. Most of `parity.rish` lives here: **140** std tests, Caravan seeds, Tally gardens, SLC-1, slcl1 receipt, crossing manifest parse harness.

**Thin edge** — the few places only the host world answers: Wayland compositor, GNOME seat, localhost UDP timing, Kaeden's live five confirmations. These stay **checkable** when the witness names the seam honestly — `slc2a_ring3_metal.rish` keeps its host visible — and the bench pin records **which metal** ran them.

A claim checkable only on Kaeden's GNOME laptop is checkable **narrowly**. That is seam honesty at work. The register weakens only when the narrow pin is omitted and the claim sounds universal.

## How a Claim Crosses In — the Doorway

The door opens one way — vision earns proof:

1. **Spark** — an image or spec names a testable claim.
2. **Scope** — one page draws the edge of complete (SLC hammock, RW contract, functional spec).
3. **Hosted proof** — smallest `.rye` or harness on metal.
4. **Witness** — `tools/*_witness.rish` or quartet block in `parity.rish`.
5. **Pin** — ROADMAP, TASKS, RTAC parity arithmetic; session log; bench tip.

**Amber** crossed this door at parity **144**. **SLC-L1** crossed at **152** for the spine claim; golden receipt `.bron` remains outside. **crossing_manifest_seed** crossed parse only — header still says PROPOSED SEED because module home is a separate claim.

Partial crossing is honest: the checkable room admits **slices** of a vision, each with its own witness, rather than pretending the whole image landed because one lap went green.

## Status Lines — Naming the Room at the Door

Every piece names its register at the top. Checkable prose uses status lines that a reader can audit:

- `Witness at parity **152**`
- `GREEN on Cursor metal `20260704.023800``
- `Research for understanding` — explicitly **not** checkable treasury advice
- `Proposed canon — seats on Kaeden's word` — not yet binding law
- `PROPOSED SEED` in source — harness green ≠ product seated

When a status line lies — green witness implied where only design exists — the rooms collapsed. The discipline is architectural: **say which room before the first argument.**

## Edge Cases — Where Teams Slip

**Compile success as product complete.** `rye build` green proves types and error sets; it does not prove lovable, complete, or shared. Scope notes hold the fuller edge.

**One green witness, many implied claims.** A parse harness does not seat a module home, a manifest law, or a crossing packet. One witness, one box checked.

**Host seam absorbed into OS story.** Wayland green proves hosted backend; it does not prove sovereign framebuffer. Name the backend in the pin.

**Mirrored pair half-done.** RW-1 at **147** needed both shells; one side green leaves the seam unbound.

**Ratings without sources.** Checkable figures cite the oracle (`openssl dgst`, pinned golden) or the survey's fresh sources — never memory.
