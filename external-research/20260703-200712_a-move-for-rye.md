## The Question

Should Rye OS carry its own version of Move — a financial-object language and model, inspired at once by TigerBeetle, by Mysticeti, and by the Sui Move object API — powering the MALA L1 and the WOV L2s? The counsel below answers in a ladder, because the two teachers in our gratitude answer the question in opposite voices, and both voices are right at their own rung.

## What Move Actually Is

Move is a language of resources. Its founding idea is **linearity**: a value of a resource type can never be copied and never silently dropped — it must be moved, whole, from one place to exactly one other place, and the compiler enforces this the way our witnesses enforce parity. Each type declares its abilities — whether it may be copied, dropped, stored, or keyed into global storage — so the safety of a value is written on the value itself. Sui's dialect then made the model object-centric: every piece of state is an object with a unique identity, a version, and an owner; transactions name their objects up front; and dynamic fields let objects grow gracefully. The language is general — anyone can define new resource types and the functions that govern them — and the linearity discipline is what keeps generality from becoming a loaded weapon.

## What TigerBeetle Holds — the Narrow Path

TigerBeetle answers the same domain from the opposite shore. It runs no user code on the hot path at all. It offers two fixed nouns — the account and the transfer — with fixed fields, fixed invariants enforced in the engine, and a small set of verbs, batched thousands to a durable write. Its bet is that double-entry primitives, done perfectly, cover the overwhelming share of real financial work better than general programmability ever will: fewer surprises, provable invariants, and performance that general contracts cannot touch. WOV, our overlay on the pinned TigerBeetle, already carries this bet into the tree under the monarch rule.

## The Counsel: a Ladder, Honestly Climbed

The two teachers braid into one ladder rather than a choice. **The floor is TigerBeetle-shaped and is already law:** fixed nouns, fixed verbs, invariants in the engine, batches to the gate — WOV holds this rung, and MALA's M-ladder climbs by demand, never by anticipation. **The horizon rung is Move-shaped and waits for proven need:** the day Linengrow's giving receipts, sanctuary records, or creator instruments genuinely outgrow the fixed schema is the day user-defined value-objects earn their tier — and Gall's Law says that day is recognized by a measured red, never by taste.

When that tier arrives, it inherits three vows from the teachers. From Move: **linearity** — a value that cannot be duplicated and cannot vanish silently is TAME's *bounds on everything* applied to value itself, and abilities are assertions the compiler keeps on our behalf. From Sui: **every object names its owner and its version**, so the system can sort owned facts from shared facts before paying for any ordering — the seam the companion piece describes. From TigerBeetle: **the verbs stay few and fixed even after the nouns multiply** — objects may define what things are, while the small audited verb set defines what may happen to them, keeping the hot path clean of user code forever.

## Names Parked for RTAC

**Pottery** opens this table, Kaeden's own offering — an object *thrown* while owned, *fired* into permanence when sealed or shared, the very journey our resins walk. The word arrives with a family the way Pond brought Puddle: *greenware* for the owned and still-moldable state, the *kiln* for the sealing, the *glaze* for the finish — aspect names waiting ready-made should the tier one day seat under this name. If the tier carries source files defining object types, **`.pott`** serves as the extension; `.pot` is set aside, since the wider world's translation tooling has long worn that suffix. A niche outside library wears the word *pottery* quietly; the whisper is faint and worth exactly this one sentence.

Asked for a best recommendation, this bench gives one plainly: **Pottery, with `.pott`.** It carries the thrown-then-fired arc in a single warm word and brings its own aspect vocabulary along. **Amphora** improves on it only where single-object precision matters most — the amphora was the sealed vessel of ancient trade, stamped with its maker's mark: thrown, fired, signed, and carried as value, the whole tier's story in one artifact. And **Grain** remains the strongest alternate for pure lexicon coherence: Rye is itself a grain, Granary already leads for the sharing surface, and granary receipts were history's first ledgers. The seat, as always, is Kaeden's alone. The full parking table, for RTAC on his word:
