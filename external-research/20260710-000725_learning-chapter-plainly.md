## In One Breath

Public learning's true outcome is a person met where they stand and carried to real capability — graduating into the regenerative economy with good work already waiting.

So the **reward** measures what neighbors can confirm: mastery demonstrated in real work, kept as an **apprenticeship portfolio** on the same open ledger every other program uses — beds raised, walls framed, books kept, tools shipped — attested by mentors, legible to any caring neighbor.

A **dignity floor** travels with every learner (and educator): a minimum held independent of pace, because the people who need the room longest often need it most. Outcome support sits above the floor.

---

## The Everyday Picture

Think of a school that already has the legal door unlocked — statutes that already let independent schools set requirements and issue diplomas, with registration light and accreditation voluntary. The working school walks through that door on day one rather than petitioning for years where the door is bolted. Gall's Law in legal form: grow from a door already open.

**Accreditation**, freely chosen, becomes a **witness suite for a school**: a standing cycle of self-examination — know yourself, show improvement as daily practice, publish what you find — the same shape as any other ledger program whose integrity outsiders can check.

The **curriculum is the economy met as apprenticeship**: soil and fiber, food and care, code and restoration — so learning, work, and place form one circulation. Graduation is an on-ramp: the portfolio walks into the work guarantee's front door; mentors come from the programs graduates join.
