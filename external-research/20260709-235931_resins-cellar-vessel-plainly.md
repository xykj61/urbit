## In One Breath

A **resin** is one content-addressed unit of stored bytes — the matter itself at a SHA3 name. The digest *is* the address and the proof.

**Amber** is **cellar software**: seal resins cold **in place** at home — signed, bit-faithful, verified by folding the catalog.

**Amphora** is **vessel software**: carry sealed cargo **in motion** across a crossing — wire, dock, or pocket — and return to a second dock when a cold scrub calls.

The **manifest** catalogs; it does not wrap the payload in an opaque bag. Tar and zip are **transport only** — never the trust boundary.

---

## The Everyday Picture

Think of a workshop with a cool cellar and a traveling amphora.

You finish a day's work and cut it into honest chunks you can name by their fingerprint. Each chunk is a **resin** — like sap set hard, labeled by what it *is*. You seal those resins into the **cellar** (Amber): they stay home, cold, verified.

When the day must travel to another dock or across a wire, you load sealed things into the **vessel** (Amphora). The vessel leaves under the same seal law and may come back for a scrub. The cellar does not walk; the vessel does not replace the cellar.

Mantra still holds the living history. Amber seals a *moment*; Amphora carries that sealed truth when place must change.

## Two Hash Widths, One Family

| Tier | Algorithm | Promise |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Working** | SHA3-256 | High-volume store units, Amber resins, hosts that pin an external oracle |
| **Canonical** | SHA3-512 | Forever-names — weave states, sealed message content identity, long-lived facts |

SHA3-256 already resists collisions beyond project scale. SHA3-512 is the wider margin where the name must mean one thing as long as the work endures — not a patch for a weak working tier.

When both appear on one log or wire, every digest eventually carries an **algorithm tag** so eras verify side by side (horizon on TASKS; intent named today).
