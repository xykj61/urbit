## The One-Sentence Claim

Run one shared physical carrier that combines what today take five separate systems — data, power, liquid fuel, nutrients, and a cultural/media channel — the way USB-C combined many cables into one, and grow it from local biological materials so it can be repaired and returned to the soil.

## What Is Load-Bearing (real, if ambitious)

- **Convergence onto one carrier** is a genuine engineering pattern with real precedent (USB-C; power-over-Ethernet; combined utility corridors). The claim that several flows can share one sheath is not exotic in principle.
- **The materials named are real research directions.** Recombinant spider silk (Bolt Threads and others) and mycelium composites both exist as materials science, not fiction. Whether they can carry power/data/fuel at infrastructure scale is unproven, and the original is honest that the specifics are speculative while the materials are real.
- **Grow-local, repair-local, return-to-soil** is a sound sovereignty and sustainability principle — it maps directly onto this fork's own "supply-chain sovereignty" value and Linengrow's civic-commerce spine.
- **Governance as its own layer, recorded on a shared readable log.** The original's "every exchange written on a shared record we can all read" is exactly this fork's append-only signed-fact log (Mantra) and its receipt model (Linengrow's SLC-L1).

## What to Read as Vision, Not Spec

- The **five-rivers framing**, the **ring-that-folds-through-itself geometry** as a life-teaching, and the **Gospel-of-Thomas epigraphs** are the piece's poetic and spiritual register. Kept in the original; set aside here.
- The **"living cable" as literally alive** is aspiration; the secular reading is "biologically-grown and biodegradable materials," which is enough to be useful without the vitalist framing.
- The **v3 "visionary edition"** wraps the same vision in Langlands/mirror-symmetry mathematics — treated in its own silo (`dual-description-design-principle.md`), since that framing is a design *metaphor* here, not a materials claim.

## What This Actually Gives This Fork

- A concrete external statement of the "one spine, many uses" principle that Rye OS already lives by (one language, one value model, one log serving kernel and product alike). The biosilk cable is that principle projected onto physical infrastructure.
- Direct reinforcement for Linengrow's civic thesis: local materials, local repair, transparent shared ledger, return-what-you-take. The original's "Silken Ground rubric" (does it nourish? does it circulate? does it return what it takes?) is a clean, citable three-question test that rhymes with this fork's own Civic lens.
- A caution worth keeping: the further a proposal drifts from testable materials into vision, the more a general reviewer needs the two clearly separated — which is the whole reason this silo exists.
