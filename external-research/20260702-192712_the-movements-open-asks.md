*The vegan and animal-advocacy world posts its needs in public every day — volunteer requests, paid gigs, and career roles across a handful of boards. This memo reads that demand directly from the sources Kaeden supplied, generalizes it into categories, and assigns each category's missions, problems, and opportunities to Rye OS module ensembles inside the Linengrow and Realidream ecosystems — with the caveats and longer horizons named beside the enthusiasm. ## What the Boards Actually Ask

**Flockwork** — the volunteer-matching board built and maintained by Vegan Hacktivists — organizes the movement's requests into eight categories that serve as our survey's spine: *Web Development & IT · Design & Multimedia · Communications & Marketing · Fundraising & Events · Outreach & Advocacy · Leadership & Operations · Research & Data · Other.* Four live requests, read whole, show the texture inside those categories:

- **A transparency engine seeking an independent audit.** A one-person, self-funded project that scores grocery and menu items on their hidden costs asks for a volunteer to audit its methodology — to make its grades defensible before peer review — and openly asks for help becoming sustainable.
- **A paid micro-gig for media.** An alternative-protein nonprofit offers a **$100 stipend** for an animator to add visuals to researcher interviews — a small, bounded, paid ask.
- **An outreach coordinator who counts.** The same nonprofit wants someone to spread template letters to legislators and **keep track of the number sent** per office — advocacy that lives or dies on honest tallies.
- **Sanctuary care software.** Vegan Hacktivists themselves seek a UI/UX lead for a platform where caregivers track feeding, cleaning, and volunteer schedules across sanctuaries worldwide — care operations as a system of record.

Around Flockwork stand the boards. **VeganJobs** runs a free, vegan-operated hub — every listing *manually reviewed*, every employer *authenticated* — with a résumé database, alerts, and volunteer and internship lanes. **VeganWork** is the aggregator: it rounds up hundreds of roles from the top boards into one searchable stream, and its live page shows the career-level spectrum — social media managers, fundraisers, campaign assistants, finance and governance leads, development specialists, operations directors, a staff veterinarian, a plant-cheese product developer, a fulfillment lead. The entrepreneur-focused and marketing-focused boards, and the careers site's board and volunteer directory, round out the field as supplied.

## The Meta-Ask: The Boards Themselves

The strongest finding is one level up. A request board *is* our architecture wearing a website: a **request** is a signed fact with skills, bounds, and a deadline; an **application** is a signed fact referencing it; an **acceptance** and a **completion receipt** close the loop; **reputation is a fold** over verified completions rather than a star rating; a **stipend is a MALA bounty** escrowed at posting; an **employer's authentication** — which the free board already performs by hand — is a Kumara-signed organizational identity checked by construction; and the **aggregator's whole job** — rounding up many boards into one stream — is Comlink serving named data across many logs, a fold instead of a scraper. The **portable résumé** the boards keep in databases becomes the person's own: verified-completion facts they carry, with selective disclosure proving *"three completed design projects for registered sanctuaries"* without exposing the whole history. This is Linengrow's civic-commerce shape meeting the movement's daily coordination need — the **Open Asks room** — and it lands on the M-ladder naturally: receipts at M1, mailable bounties at M2, reputation folds throughout.

## Caveats, Held in the Light

Four, firmly. **Do not capture what already works** — Flockwork and the boards are gifts to the movement, built by allies; our first lap *interoperates* (publish to them, read from them, offer receipts beneath them) rather than competing for their communities. **Consent before counting** — advocacy tallies and volunteer histories touch real people's political and personal lives; aggregate folds and zero-knowledge disclosure are requirements, never features. **The economics stay honest** — free boards run on donated labor; a receipts-and-bounties layer must lower their costs, never tax their users. **And the reward must point at the good** — CIVIC's first principle applies to us most of all: measure completed care, verified audits, letters truly sent, rather than engagement.

## Longer Horizons and Conclusions

Nearest: the **Open Asks room** enters Linengrow's design ladder as a candidate first application beside the receipt lap, once SLC-L1's scope note draws its edge — design only, after the doors and the Amber season. Middle: the **sanctuary care log** as the flagship public-benefit deployment — the category where our grain (signed care facts, bounded budgets, dignity floors) serves beings who cannot check the ledger themselves. Far: the movement's whole coordination layer — requests, receipts, reputations, bounties — as one portable, person-owned graph, with today's boards as honored peers on it rather than silos beside it. The conclusion the reading forces is gentle and large: **the movement is already asking, in a hundred small posts, for exactly the trust infrastructure we are building — and the kindest bridge is to serve their categories one verified receipt at a time.**
