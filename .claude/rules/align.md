# "align" / "align our direction" — the reconciliation pass

When Keaton says **"align"** or **"align our direction,"** walk the compass rose and reconcile the plan with what is actually true, then update the plan accordingly. Where "follow our compass" is the *return habit*, **align** is the *reconcile action*.

## What "align" does, in order

1. **Walk the compass rose** (per `foundations/20260706-185112_follow-our-compass.md`): Foundations (`foundations/README.md`) → Grain (`active-designing/20260702-184312_the-grain-and-the-crossing.md`) → Two Rooms (`context/TWO_ROOMS.md`) → Active-designing (`active-designing/README.md`) → Now (`work-in-progress/TASKS.md`) → Order (`work-in-progress/ROADMAP.md`).
2. **Check the current work against ROADMAP and TASKS** — is it on the plan, ahead of a gate, or superseded by a green witness the plan hasn't caught up to?
3. **Reconcile, brief and green witnesses winning** — fix the plan to match reality (mark landed laps, update now/next, reposition stale ordering per accrete-never-break; never delete dated records).
4. **Name the one aligned next step plainly** — honoring the finishing edge (ship one real thing before naming the next; `counsel/20260715-190000_the-design-over-build-ratio-and-finishing.md`).

## What align always keeps

- **Witness before narrative** — no lap marked "landed" without a green witness (or an honest note why it could not run).
- **Accrete, never break** — reconciling repositions and re-dates; it never rewrites dated records.
- **The finishing edge** — lean toward *build the next real thing*, not *name the next idea*, when the design-over-build ratio has tipped.
- **A record rides along** — a plan-changing align pass writes a session log and, on Keaton's word, ships (`send` rule).

## Why the word exists

Direction frays as a tree grows fast; "align" stops, re-reads the compass, checks the plan against what actually ran, and points at the single next step worth taking — one calm, honest answer grounded in green witnesses rather than enthusiasm.
