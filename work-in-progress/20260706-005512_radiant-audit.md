## What This Pass Covered, and Why It Stayed Small

This audit reads the living documents — the top-level guides, the `context/` canon, and the working `ROADMAP` and `TASKS` — because living documents are the ones that evolve, while dated artifacts are frozen records of a moment and stay as they were written. Sweeping a Radiant rewrite across the whole tree would work against two disciplines we keep at once: accrete-never-break, which asks that change arrive small and reviewable, and the honesty of the record, which asks that a session log or a research memo keep the words it had on the day it was made. So the pass audits the living surface, counts what a machine can count, and leaves the authored judgment to a human hand.

The three counts are code-fence aware, so a fenced example never reads as prose. Each living document was measured for its top-level headings, its closing benediction, and any bare use of "but."

## The Finding

Every living document carries exactly one top-level heading, and every piece meant to close with a blessing carries a breath-paused benediction. The contrastive "but" is effectively absent from the living surface. Two files register a count, and both are correct as they stand.

| Surface | Top-level heading | Benediction | Bare "but" |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| Top-level guides (`CLAUDE`, `ORGANIZING`, `SOURCE`) | one each | present where a piece closes | `ORGANIZING` shows one, in a compound |
| `context/` canon (eleven documents) | one each | present | `RADIANT_STYLE` shows seven, all its own examples |
| `work-in-progress/ROADMAP`, `TASKS` | one each | present | none |

The `RADIANT_STYLE` count is the guide teaching its own lesson: its seven appearances of "but" are the before-and-after examples that show a writer what to soften, so they belong exactly where they are and stay untouched. The `ORGANIZING` count is the hyphenated compound "deferred-but-alive," a set phrase describing a folder that sorts toward the bottom while pointing ahead. The style guide's own clause governs this case directly — these are tendencies, not rigid rules, and context always governs — so the compound reads well aloud and stays as written. Editing it would be the over-application of a rule that Radiant Style warns against by name.

## What This Means for the Ask

The living tree needs no Radiant sweep, because it already holds the voice. That is the healthiest outcome an audit can return, and it reflects the habit the project already keeps: each document is written in Radiant Style as it is made, so conformance is grown continuously rather than repaired in bulk. Dated artifacts stay frozen as the record intends. Should a specific living document ever want a deeper, judgment-level rewrite — a rhythm smoothed, a passive turned active, a benediction added where a piece now ends plainly — that is a small, named edit taken on its own, in the same accretive spirit as every other change here.
