## The Decision, Stated First

1. **Dexter is an aspect of the Brushstroke / Sala (Landscape) stack** — the acting hand beside the drawn surface — not an aspect of Dill, and not of Pool.
2. **Fuse Dexter with Glow on GrapheneOS** as the next named *product* lap when Keaton opens it: NativeActivity input → Dexter's pure bounded core → fold / shell, still Glow/Rye → native.
3. **Do not nest Dill inside Dexter.** Do not rename Dexter to Dill. Dill stays an unfilled vane gap until a real Dill-parallel revival earns its own birth under this folder.

## Why This Is Reviving, Not Designing Alone

- The **elder that already works** is Dexter's line editor and the glass that already paints on Pixel. The revival grows a GrapheneOS-facing *consumer* of that same pure input shape beside the Wayland drain — not a rewrite of Android, and not a Kotlin shim.
- The **elder gap** is Dill. Naming it here prevents a false revival (calling Dexter "Dill" to feel complete). Reviving replaces renaming (`../context/specs/20260714-004428_reviving-replaces-renaming.md`).

## The Discipline This Revival Keeps

- **Name the elders:** Dexter core · Dill gap · NativeActivity glass.
- **Grow beside, never break:** `window_input.rye` keeps its home and witnesses; glass pack stays green; Dill is not faked into existence.
- **Born with Glow names:** the glass input path stays **Dexter**; any future terminal vane is born with its own Glow-direction name when opened.
- **Supersede only when proven:** Dexter-on-glass earns green witnesses (device-free + pack + Pixel look) before any claim that "input is closed on GrapheneOS."
- **TAME through emitted Rye:** Dexter stays bounded (`max_line_len`), named refuses past the bound, no hidden allocation. Glow rune work stays on `glow/README.md` as working surface; foundations and TAME stay the north star. A Glow-specific TAME supplement waits until distinct checkable Glow idioms exist beyond lowering to Rye.
