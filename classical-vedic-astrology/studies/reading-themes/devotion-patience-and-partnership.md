## Beginning where her chart begins: fidelity

Alice's mind and wisdom both rest under Aryaman, the keeper of promises — so it feels right to begin here. Aryaman's whole teaching is that **trust comes first**, and that the kept promise is the soil in which every beautiful thing grows. Fidelity, truthfulness, devotion, and patience are not constraints on love; they are what make love deep, safe, and lasting. This is the thread that runs through everything below.

## The student season: a gathered, undivided heart

In her present season — the student years — the tradition counsels **temperance**. The old word for this is *celibacy*, yet that word names only what is set aside; the warmer and truer name is **patient wholeness**, or the **undivided heart**. The idea is gentle and affirmative: during these years, love's bright energy is gathered and kept whole rather than scattered, held in patient reserve, so that in time it can be given fully and freely to one devoted bond. A river kept to one channel runs deep; a flame kept in one lamp burns steady and bright.

This counsel fits the age we live in. The Bhāgavata tradition describes the present age as one where desires run scattered and dharma grows thin — and so it counsels gathering and steadying as the wise response, rather than dispersal. For Alice this lands with special grace, because it is exactly the lesson her airy Venus is already learning: to move from the light and the many toward the deep and the devoted, from charm toward presence, from novelty toward patience. The temperance of the student years is simply that same soul lesson, practiced early and kindly.

## Two honorable paths onward

As the student season gives way to the householder season — near the age of twenty-five, or after her first complete Saturn return around twenty-nine — two beautiful and fully honorable paths open before her. Either is whole. Either is worthy.

**The first is the path of the apsara** — the celestial dancer, given to beauty, art, and grace. This is a devoted, disciplined, single-hearted life lived for creative and spiritual flourishing, free and luminous, without children and without need of marriage. For a heart as airy, communicative, and artistic as hers, this path is genuinely open and genuinely complete. To give one's life to beauty and to the making of beautiful things, with discipline and devotion, is a noble way to live.

**The second is the path of the householder** — a devoted, faithful, exclusive partnership, entered as the householder season begins, and the family life that may grow from it. For a heart that also carries the steady, bearing depth of her Moon, this path offers its own deep fulfillment: the long bond, patiently tended, that Aryaman's fidelity is made for. If she walks this path, the tradition counsels **one devoted partner**, cherished and trusted for life — exactly the depth that the patient, faithful path rewards.

She need not choose now. She need only know that both doors are open, and that whichever she walks, her chart gives her what it asks: the artist's grace for the one, the bearer's devotion for the other, and a contented, secure heart for both.

## On welcoming children, should she walk the household path

If Alice chooses partnership and the wish for children arises, the tradition — read with a loving eye toward our crowded, warming world — offers one more piece of gentle counsel: to consider **welcoming home a child already here**. Adoption is not a lesser way of becoming a parent; it is a radiant one. It expands a family through love rather than through addition, and it is a double gift — a home for a child who needs one, and a kindness to an Earth already carrying much.

This counsel rests on a clear-eyed insight. Teachers of our own time, among them Acharya Prashant, have pointed out that choosing one fewer biological child spares the planet on the order of sixty tons of carbon each year — far more than a lifetime of tree-planting can absorb — and that caring for the Earth's health begins with the wisdom to live, and to grow our families, with a lighter hand. Offered in the same breath as veganism and a gentle footprint, it is simply love extended outward: to a child, to the creatures, to the living world. And it asks nothing of devotion to a spouse; the dedication to one's partner stays whole and warm. This is a perspective to weigh with an open heart, never a rule — yet it is a beautiful one, and worth holding up to the light.

## Choosing a partner, with a wise guide

Should she walk the household path, the tradition gives one more warm piece of guidance, and it returns us to Aryaman: choose a partner who keeps promises. A wise teacher-guide — one who lives the path themselves — can help her see clearly here, beyond the first spark, to the deeper indicators of a bond that will last.

Look for someone who lives the five tenets of a devoted life: **ahimsa**, a compassionate, non-violent path expressed as veganism and care for the Earth; **bhakti**, devotion to something higher than the self; **dhyana**, the steadiness of meditation; **nada**, a love of sacred sound and deep listening; and **shastra**, the study of wisdom. And beyond the principles, look for the plain good signs of a life-companion: someone **dedicated and hard-working**, **fun and warm**, and **devoted for the long road** — the kind of person whose word, like Aryaman's, can be trusted across all the seasons of a life.

There is an old and lovely teaching that a person who is supported, secure, and happy radiates that happiness to everyone around them — so that the home where joy is cherished flourishes, and the home where it is neglected withers. To seek the support and steadiness that let you flourish, then, is not selfishness; it is generosity, because your flourishing blesses your whole household. A good partnership, on either side, is built of exactly this: each tending the other's happiness, so that joy flows freely through the home.

## Holding it all lightly

These are seasons and possibilities, offered with love — not a fate written in the stars. Alice's freedom is total, her discernment her own, and a wise guide a gift along the way. Whichever path she walks, the heart of it stays simple and bright: keep your word, cherish what you love, move with patience, and let devotion be the thread.
