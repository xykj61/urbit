# The Three-Pass Opposition — Beginning, Reckoning, Conclusion

**Stamp:** `20260705.172312 UDT` · **Style:** Radiant, gentle register
**Method:** Siloed — distilled in our own voice; teacher named with gratitude below

---

Now and then a moving planet forms an opposition to another and, because of the retrograde loop, the exact opposition arrives three times rather than once — the planet reaches the point going forward, backs over it in retreat, and crosses it a third time going forward again. When this happens, the three passes are not three repetitions of one blow. They are three movements of a single lesson: a beginning that raises the theme, a reckoning that turns it inward, and a conclusion that resolves it. To read the three-pass rhythm as one whole is to hand a person a difficulty that already contains its own resolution.

## The first pass — the theme arrives

The opening crossing brings the matter to the surface. Something is encountered — a limit, a lack, a truth about a bond or a longing that had been avoided. It can feel abrupt, since it is the moment the season names its subject. The first pass is best met as an announcement rather than a sentence: this is what these months are about; take note, and do not yet rush to fix it. What surfaces here is the raw material the rest of the arc will work.

## The second pass — the inward reckoning

When the planet turns retrograde and crosses the point a second time, the lesson goes inward. Retrograde motion is review by nature, so this middle pass is the reckoning — the quiet, often uncomfortable turning-over of what the first pass raised. Here a person examines their own part, their attachments, the story they have been telling themselves. Little is resolved outwardly in this movement; the work is interior. It asks honesty more than action, and patience most of all, because the reckoning cannot be hurried and should not be.

## The third pass — the conclusion

The final forward crossing brings resolution. What was raised and then reckoned with can now be integrated, set down, or completed. The third pass is where a person arrives at the other side of the lesson carrying something they did not have at the first — usually some measure of acceptance, and often the ability to release what they had been gripping. The arc that began with an encounter ends with an integration, and the difficulty, seen whole, turns out to have been a passage rather than a punishment.

## When limit meets longing

The rhythm speaks most tenderly when the disciplining power opposes the loving one across the discerning and the dissolving signs, for then the three passes teach, over their season, to let be and to let go. The first pass meets the ache of limit or lack in love. The second turns inward to the attachment beneath the ache. The third releases it, and what remains is a quieter, freer heart. Learning to let it be, and then to let it go, is precisely the gift this three-beat lesson is built to give.

## Reading it in a chart

The craft is to refuse to read a single hard transit as one isolated strike. When the retrograde loop is present, name the three passes and their dates, and describe the arc as beginning, reckoning, and conclusion, so the person can pace themselves through it. Counsel patience at the first pass, honesty at the second, and release at the third. Framed this way, even a demanding transit becomes bearable, because the person can see that a resolution is built into its very shape, waiting at the far end of the third crossing.
