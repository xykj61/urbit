## The zodiac's human signs — who stands upright in the wheel

Look at the zodiac symbols, the teacher invites: ram, bull, then **Gemini** — the first **human** figure. Crab, lion, then **Virgo**, Mercury's second human sign. **Libra** is not a mere scale; it is a woman holding scales — Venus's human, airy sign (her other sign is the bull). Scorpio, centaur-or-archer, makara, **Aquarius** another human under Saturn, then fish. Mostly the human signs belong to **Mercury**, then **Venus**, then **Saturn** a little — the liberal political group in the teacher's symbolic map.

## Two political groups — not a ballot, a temperament map

Astrology names two broad temperamental camps in society, rendered here **with equal dignity** — observations about nature, not endorsements:

**Conservatives** (fire and water — more extreme, fixed in quality): **Sun** as king of conservatives; **Moon**; **Mars**; **Jupiter** as religion, faith, police, military, royalty, leadership. Their attitude: *this is how it is; this is how it shall be.* Regularity, predictability, repetition — even necessary boringness — is their gift.

**Liberals** (earth and air — mid-gendered, flexible): **Saturn** as the regular person, the proletariat, the blue-collar worker; **Venus** as love, peace, beauty, art — expressive by nature; **Mercury** as poets, scientists, intellect, wit, dance, communication. Their attitude: society progresses through human creativity, health (Virgo), and the lightness of Gemini's dance.

Neither camp is villainized in the teaching; each carries a necessary social function.

## Mercury and Jupiter — the two bridges

**Mercury** is technically liberal yet **bipartisan**: scientists funded by government, schools tied to state — intellect serving both camps, finding similarity and workable compromise. **Jupiter** plays the mirror role from the conservative side: religion and absolute trust on one hand, philosophy and morals on the other — explaining law so people do not revolt, yet also seeking Mercury's help to make rules intelligible.

Both planets own **dual signs** alone among the seven classical lights: Mercury holds Gemini and Virgo; Jupiter holds Sagittarius and Pisces — because both are pluralistic, bridge-building, thinking across camps.

## The nodes — a third group, friendly to neither throne

**Rahu and Ketu** fit neither camp cleanly. They lean friendly toward liberals yet **attack Sun and Moon** — the conservative luminaries. The teacher compares them to a punk subculture: neither satisfied with liberal wishy-washiness nor conservative rigidity; wanting a revolution beyond pretend democracy, toward a truer anarchy. They facilitate the **cycle** by which ideal Mercury–Jupiter visions rise, become Sun–Saturn practicality, stagnate, and must be recycled.

## Saturn's slow power — and who decides revolt

**Saturn** is the mass of people — slow to move, yet ultimately more powerful than any king once coordinated. **Mercury and Jupiter together**, the teacher says, play the delicate role of deciding whether society has gone too far: whether to inspire allegiance to the ruler or revolt among artists and workers. Kings remain only while people empower them.

Yet the sad pattern of history: even when Mercury and Jupiter discern rightly, implementers become Sun and Saturn — revolutions become empires; communism in theory becomes authoritarian in practice; spiritual founders become popes and wars. Cycles renew; utopia never stays fixed. That is neither curse nor blessing — it is the wheel.

## Symbolic riches — Libra, liberty, the human signs

Libra's art — Lady Justice, the Statue of Liberty — encodes **liberty** in the very word *Liberal*. The human signs (Gemini's dancing pair, Virgo's health, Libra's balance) are described as engines of social progress; conservative fire and water hold extremity and order. Evolution of culture, in this symbolic reading, flows from the liberal camp's creativity — yet always under pressure from monarchic authority.

## A chart application — when temperament meets the native sky

A chart strong in Mercury, Venus, or Saturn may show a person naturally liberal in the teacher's symbolic sense — expressive, intellectual, or populist. Strong Sun, Moon, Mars, or Jupiter may show conservative temperament — leadership, faith, regularity, command. Rahu or Ketu prominent may show the outsider revolutionary streak — uncomfortable in either camp. These are lenses for understanding tendency, never labels for judgment in worldly politics.
