This file holds both the *how* of her Mars, its nature in Leo, and a closing word on the *where*, its work in the 10th. ## Fire welcomed by fire

Mars stands in Leo as a commander in the king's own court — fire welcomed by fire, will given a throne. Mars is the planet of action, courage, and force; Leo is the domain of the Sun, ruled by a friend, lit and fixed and noble. Here Mars is strong, and more than strong — it is dignified. The restless drive that elsewhere rushes ahead of itself arrives, in Leo, already knowing where it is going.

## The noble will

Call it the noble will. Mars in Leo does not act coldly, nor merely to win; it acts from a center, with a sense of honor woven through the deed. It protects. It leads by example. It fights for what it loves rather than against the ones it loves. Because Leo and the Sun are so bound up with the self, Alice's action here is not a separate thing she does — it becomes part of who she is. There is a hero in this placement, and the hero's bravery is worth trusting precisely because it rises from a secure inner place rather than from fear.

## Fire as dharma

Fire is the element of dharma — of purpose, integrity, and right action — and three fires meet in this star: Mars, Leo, and the Sun behind them both. So this will is meant to serve something. Mars supplies the courage to act; Leo gives that courage a stable identity and a clear direction; and the action, at its best, becomes a beacon rather than a weapon. The old sky-watchers placed Regulus, the heart of the lion, at the center of this sign and called it a royal star, a guardian of cosmic order. That is the high form of Alice's Mars: not the reckless soldier, but the commander who receives his orders from the king and wields his strength responsibly.

## An honest note on the shadow

The lessons that come with such power are honest ones, and worth meeting early. This placement can quietly believe that *I am what I do* — that worth must be re-earned through every action, that the spotlight must never fade. It can hunger for respect and applause, and ache when met with indifference. The medicine is to remember that the fire is already hers; she does not have to perform to keep it lit. Strength learns its deepest lesson in the moments it cannot win — in patience, in surrender, in knowing when not to fight. Even a born leader puts a hand in the mud. The Bhāgavata's way is the clean one here: act with all one's courage, offer the fruit, and let the doing be enough.

## Mars in the 10th house

Alice's Mars shares the 10th house with her Sun — the highest and most public sector of the chart, the open noon sky where one is fully seen. This doubles the fire of her visible life: a strong drive toward career, contribution, and a purpose carried out in the world's view. The 10th is a house of movement, of climbing and reaching, and Mars loves to move. Held with poise, this makes her a courageous and capable presence — a leader who strengthens others. The counsel is gentle and steady: let her action protect and uplift; lead in a way that inspires rather than controls; and keep stillness at the center of so much fire, the way the lion's heart stays calm beneath its strength.
