## The Sun at home in its own sign

The Sun rules Leo, and a ruler is at ease in the house he himself has built. Here the Sun comes home. Whatever the Sun means — the soul, the central self, the steady seat of awareness — Leo welcomes and amplifies. There is little friction in this meeting; the qualities of the sign and the qualities of the light agree, and from that agreement comes a clear, warm sense of self. This is among the most harmonious places the Sun can sit.

## The gift: confidence that quietly draws others

Confidence is the first gift, and it is quieter than it sounds. Confidence here means self-direction — Alice knows her own mind without waiting to be told. It means self-assurance — she can rest in herself without the gnawing of doubt. This is what makes a Leo Sun magnetic: not a particular beauty, but the ease of a person at home in herself. People are drawn to that ease.

From self-assurance flows decisiveness, and from decisiveness, the capacity to lead. Leadership, at root, is simply the willingness to decide — to carry the weight of choosing when others would rather be carried. A Leo Sun can hold that weight, and others come to rely on it.

## Loyalty and steadiness

Leo is a fixed sign, and the Sun is the most faithful body in the sky — rising each day at its appointed place, keeping the year's tempo without fail. Together they give loyalty, reliability, a steadiness others lean on. A Leo Sun shows up. People learn to set their rhythm by such a person.

## The deeper note: a self that can know itself

The Sun is the self, and Leo is the self's own kingdom, so Alice's whole life carries a strong interest in the self. Held lightly, this becomes self-knowledge — consciousness turned toward its own source, the lamp that lights its own standing place. With devotion and study alongside it, that same light can open inward toward the Self the Bhāgavata Purāṇa points to, the soul behind the soul. The work of these years is to keep the flame warm rather than proud, so that strength serves rather than displays.

## An honest note on the shadow

Every brightness has its quiet cost, and honesty serves Alice better than flattery. A Sun pressed too hard can grow domineering, slow to follow, impatient with others' small faults, quick to over-commit. These are not her fate; they are simply the places to keep a little humility. The medicine is gentle — temperance, and the memory that even the king kneels.
