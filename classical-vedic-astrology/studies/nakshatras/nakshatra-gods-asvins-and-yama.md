## Two brothers, children of the Sun

Alice's birth day belongs to two neighboring stars, and the gods of those two stars are brothers — sons of the Sun. The first star belongs to the shining twins. The second belongs to the fair judge. Their stories sit side by side, and so do their gifts.

## The shining twins

The twins arrived in the world all at once, swift and bright, born of the Sun and his radiant companion while both wore the form of horses. From their first breath they carried light, beauty, and speed. People called them the doctors of the gods, for they were the most gifted healers anywhere — they could restore youth to the old, bring sight to the unseeing, and mend what seemed past mending. They were so skilled that they could lift away a weary head and set a fresh one gently in its place, then make the body whole again, good as new. They also taught people one of the kindest skills of all: how to find water in a dry season, how to dig wells and bring the streams to the fields.

The twins loved to run free. They loved beauty, health, and good company, and a little playful mischief followed them everywhere. They were never solemn. They were the bright morning of things.

## How the twins won their seat

For a while, the twins were kept from the special, strengthening nectar that the gods shared at their table. The king of the gods was a worried sort, and he fretted that two such bright newcomers might one day outshine him, so he asked them to wait outside.

The twins did not sulk, and they did not force their way in. They did something cleverer and warmer: they used the very gifts they already had. They sought out a wise old keeper of secrets and offered their healing art in fair exchange for his knowledge. When a threat hung over that wise one, the twins simply healed their way around it — lifting his head to safety, teaching through a horse's head set gently in its place, and afterward restoring his own, all whole again. Later they met a sage grown very old, married to a young woman of great devotion, and for her sake they returned him to radiant youth and full health. In deep thanks, he helped the twins at last to their rightful seat at the table. And here is the heart of it: the other gods *liked* the twins. Because the twins had shown their gifts openly and warmly, they had gathered friends, and the friends spoke up for them, and the king lifted his ban. The twins took their rightful place among the gods, exactly the bright ones they had always been.

Woven into that story are the two quiet lessons of their star. The twins had real gifts — and they had the courage and warmth to *use* those gifts out in the world, among people, rather than hiding them away. That openness drew companions to them, and no one wins everything alone. So the way of the shining twins is this: find the good you carry, show it generously, and gather the friends whose gifts complete your own.

## The fair judge

The twins' brother carries a very different weight. When the worried king took the throne, he could not see all things from every side the way the old, all-seeing keeper of order once had. So that power of complete, fair sight passed to the brother — along with a faithful record-keeper who notices everything, and a cord for gently drawing each soul to account.

This made the brother the great judge — and a fine one, because he is perfectly fair. He belongs to no team and plays no favorites. He does not care who is grand and who is humble; he looks only at what was actually done. His name means *discipline* and *restraint*, and that is closer to his true nature than any darker title. He is not cruel, and he does not make sad things happen. He is the one who makes sure that every action meets its honest fruit, so that the world stays fair and good things are protected and can flourish. His is a heavy, serious, deeply responsible task, and he bears it without flinching. That steady bearing of weight is the very gift of his star.

His discipline always serves something tender. A loving parent who says *put on your jacket, it is cold* is not being harsh; the small rule is love, protecting something precious. So it is with the fair judge. The boundaries are never the point. The precious thing they protect is always the point.

## Two gifts, side by side

The shining twins give vitality, beauty, swiftness, and the warm gift of drawing people together. The fair judge gives discipline, fairness, fortitude, and the strength to carry real weight. Alice's day opened under the first and matured into the second — and in the file on her lunar day and birth-star, we follow how beautifully those two gifts pass the baton, one to the next.

## With Gratitude

These stories are drawn from the long Vedic and Puranic tradition. The episode of the youthful restoration of the old sage and his devoted young wife is told in the *Srimad Bhagavatam* (Canto 9); other threads come from the wider Vedic literature. We have retold them in our own gentle words, with the values of the *Bhagavatam* held in view — every being a spark of the divine, and devotion the quiet thread through all of it. The reading of the star-lessons within these tales — the twins' two secrets, the judge's protective discipline — we received with gratitude from the storytelling of **Vic DiCara**, whose work on the nakshatra teachings lit this path. Any sentence here that sounds like the source has not yet been fully made our own; the understanding is what we keep, and the teachers are named here with warmth.
