## Reframe — Rye already runs with no OS

Aurora is freestanding Rye on bare metal; the tree’s width policy names that lane. Tonight’s C is not “Rye can’t go low.” Rye boots on *nothing*.

The narrower question: **why can’t Rye be a Genode component today?**

### Why Genode wants the teacher’s C shape

A Genode component is not a program that makes syscalls. It is a program that **links a runtime**. On Linux the kernel door is language-agnostic — numbers in registers; Zig emits them as happily as C. Genode offers components no such door. The component receives its world — capabilities, environment, the session that carries `printf` to serial — through Genode’s base and libc *libraries*, entering through the libc port’s construct hook. The runtime is the library; to stand on the seat you must link it under the ABI the seat expects.

Zig has no `genode` target:
- aim at `linux` → syscall numbers that mean nothing to nova
- aim at `freestanding` → a blob with no parent protocol — **Aurora’s lane**, not Genode’s

The teacher says ordinary distro compilers fail its requirements (TLS among them) — hence the patched GCC. An untested compiler under the rung would plant an unverified ABI seam where the claim lives.

### TAME ordered the choice

The proven-seat ladder proves the *seat* with the fewest unproven variables. Safety votes first: teacher toolchain + teacher entry shape is the tested pair.

Look at what the C is — some forty lines, two checks, four prints. **Seam C.** Every gram of meaning wore Rye: Kumara made keys and signature; a Rye generator wrote the fixture; Rishi witnesses pin and orchestrate; the red-avoid lint is ours. C carried two function calls across an ABI gap — the same law that lets `sh` live at POSIX seams. The seam is not the model.
