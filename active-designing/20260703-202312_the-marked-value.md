## One Idea, Worn on the Face

Every value that crosses a seam wears a mark naming its type. That is the whole idea, and it unifies things this tree already does separately. A `.brix` descriptor announces what each brick *is*. A `.rish` result arrives as a structured value. The shoulder line of the crossing vessel names its cargo in plain words. A resin in the cellar should be knowable without breaking its seal. What these share is a small worn sign — the way a mark on the forehead tells at a glance what one is and how one is to be received. The candidate word for this sign is **Tilak**, parked in RTAC with Titulus, Emblem, and Motif beside it; the prose below says *type-mark* until Kaeden speaks.

## The Three Duties of a Mark

Every type-mark keeps exactly three duties, and their candidate names came home in the earlier naming harvest. **Absorb**: make a value of this type from another type, where the making is honest — a plain record absorbed from text, a frame absorbed from a drawing list. **Express**: render this type as another — a record expressed as text for a person, a source file expressed as lines for tending. **Tend**: care for this type's history — the diff, the patch, the merge, done in the type's own grain rather than in blind bytes. A mark with no conversions is still a whole mark; absorb and express pairs are earned one at a time, when a real crossing needs them, never speculatively — the growth law applied to converters.

## Marks Are Values in the Weave

A mark definition is itself a value: named, content-addressed, stored in Weave like everything else, and carried as cargo when it must travel. Two marks alone are hardcoded in the engine, breaking the circle of who-validates-the-validators honestly: **the plain-bytes mark**, which every value trivially satisfies, and **the manifest mark**, so a vessel can always be read. Everything above those two roots is defined in the tree, versioned by Mantra, and accretes without breaking: a mark once published keeps its meaning forever, new marks arrive beside old ones, and absorb-express bridges carry values between generations.

## Where the Mark Lands Across the Design

This section is the unification the round asked for, seam by seam. **The crossing vessel (Amphora — vessel software)**: every manifest entry now carries three fields — its type-mark, its digest at the tiers the resins law fixes, and its name — and the shoulder line speaks the cargo's marks in plain words, so a person holding the vessel knows what rides inside before any seal is broken. **Comlink**: each lane declares the marks it carries — the fact lane carries signed-event marks, the stream lane carries frame marks — so the sea itself knows its cargo classes and refuses a stranger at the rail. **Weave and Mantra**: tending goes typed — line-tending for source, record-tending for descriptors, whole-value tending for sealed things — so history becomes aware of what it holds, and a merge in a descriptor stops pretending to be a merge in prose. **Pond**: the customs house applies policy *per mark* at receipt — what may be placed, what may be run, what waits for a word — before anything touches the floor. **Brix**: the descriptor's comment already says what each brick is; the mark makes that sentence machine-checkable, one honest field. **Bron**, as proposed, is itself a mark — the letter's type — which is perhaps the cleanest sentence yet written about its duty. **Amber (cellar software)**: every resin records the marks of what it seals, so a reader in another decade knows what the cellar held without opening it — the cellar's promise kept even to strangers.

## The Vows, Extended

The crossing's vows extend naturally to types. Validation happens at every boundary: the digest is checked twice and the mark is checked at receipt, both sides of the seam guarded, because the boundary between valid and invalid is where the faults live. An unknown mark is refused whole — the wreck rule for types; there is no partial trust of cargo whose kind cannot be named. And marks accrete, never break: renames come with bridges, retirements come with seasons, and no published meaning is ever silently rewritten.

## The Laps

Lap one: the plain-bytes mark, the manifest mark, and the three-field manifest line — used first by the loop's own nightly packet, whose proposed seed rides beside this writing in work-in-progress, unverified until a bench raises and a witness runs red then green. Lap two: source marks for the tree's own tongues, with typed tending arriving in Mantra where it pays first. Lap three: absorb-express pairs, each earned by a real crossing that needs it, each landing with its witness. every lap is simple, lovable, and complete at its own size, and no lap claims green before the bench reports it.
