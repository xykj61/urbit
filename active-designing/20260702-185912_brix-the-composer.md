## What Brix Is

Brix is the composing language of Rye OS: the thing that says what a system is made of, and how its parts lawfully combine. A `.brix` description declares, arranges, and composes; it does not store, version, or run. Its vocabulary is settled — a **brick** is one composable unit, a **course** a row of declared parts, a **bond** the lawful rule connecting two bricks into a larger one, and **lay** the act of placing a brick in a course. Brix stacks; it never weaves. Weaving is Mantra's honest work, within one file's history.

## The Pattern, in Our Own Words

The clarity this brief holds arrived through a crossing — the declarative lineage was studied, named, and thanked in its own room — and what crossed the doorway is one pattern, restated on the grain: **a whole system is a value.** Its parts are declared, each part is held by the name of its own content, changes arrive through one lawful override rather than a module system, and the running services are supervised children of the same declared whole. Every strand of that sentence is already ours.

| Concern | Module | Role |
|---------|--------|------|
| Declare what the system is made of | **Brix** | a course of bricks |
| Combine and override, lawfully | the **bond** in Brix | one small combinator, uniformly applied |
| Hold each brick, immutable | **Tablecloth** | content-addressed by digest, written once |
| Weave the history of what changed | **Mantra** | the projection over the log |
| Move bricks between machines | **Comlink** | sealed at identity, signature on every artifact |
| Supervise what a course declares | **Caravan** | the capability table names what each child may do |
| Bound the memory beneath | **Tally** | gardens under every part |
| Wake the machine into the whole | **Aurora** | boot ends where supervision begins |
| Carry the data a `.brix` speaks | **Bron** | the notation between modules |

## The Package System Is an Ensemble, Not One Module

The package manager is Brix at the head of a small ensemble, each part single-stranded. A `.brix` description, written in Bron notation, evaluates through its lawful bond into a course of bricks — a closure of exactly what the system needs. Those bricks live content-addressed in Tablecloth, named by the digest of their content. Their history is woven by Mantra. They travel between machines over Comlink, every artifact signed and every transfer sealed identity to identity — distribution on a public-key model rather than anonymous fetching, so an artifact is what its digest says and comes from whom it claims. The services the course declares are supervised by Caravan; Tally bounds the memory; Aurora boots the machine into Caravan, which brings the declared system to life. Brix is precisely the *language of composition and override*; the *package manager* is the whole ensemble; and the boundary keeps each module about one thing.

## Two Divergences, On Purpose

First, distribution is sealed: where the lineage we studied trusts anonymous caches, Rye OS distributes under signature at identity — trust by construction, carried from the kernel up into the package system. Second, the ground is ours: Brix composes for Rye OS — Caravan supervising, Aurora booting, one value model rooted in a keypair — so the whole picture is the studied pattern reimagined on our append-only, signed-facts spine.

## How It Grows

Brix is small today: a `.brix` descriptor with its tracked bricks, read by Mantra. It grows by the same law as everything here. The first lap is a course evaluating into a closure of content-addressed bricks in Tablecloth, arriving after the Amber season gives sealing its witnessed shape — the packaging tool-aspect and the toolset ladder already name the rungs. Nothing is built ahead of the seed it composes.
