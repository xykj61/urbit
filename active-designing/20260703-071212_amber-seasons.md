## One Word, Three Rooms

The bench did not borrow this word tonight; it has been growing here all along.

In the **garden's room**, a season is a memory region's whole life: opened with a stated budget,
filled within its edges, and cleared whole in one gesture when the work completes. Every redraw
of the drawn surface is a season — the frame garden opens at the start of render and returns
everything at submit. The hosted arena is the same shape at process scale: a single region,
released at the end of its season, rather than a thousand small frees the reader must trust.

In the **bench's room**, a season is a stretch of working time given to one module's laps — the
Amber season the ladder named, the mirrored-pair-per-season audit rule, the later seasons where
settlement and markets wait, even the voice itself, held for this season until Kaeden restores
Reya 2.

Both rooms share one shape, and Amber's room inherits it exactly: **a season is a bounded
interval with a named opening, a stated budget, and a clean close that returns everything it
held.** The garden bounds space this way. The bench bounds attention this way. Amber bounds
*custody* this way — a season is Tally's discipline applied to time.

## Amber Sleeps Between Seasons

Amber is **cellar software** — preservation in place. A **season** is how the cellar opens and closes: deliberate custody at home. When resins must reach a second dock, **Amphora** — **vessel software** — carries them in motion; that pour is a later lap, not a daemon waking the cellar.

Amber's resting state is the whole point of Amber. Bare platters lie sleeved and shelved,
unpowered and unplugged, one rotating set away from the house entirely. The catalog — the pure
fold over the archive log — stays readable warm in the working tree without a single drive
docked. The master key stays cold and certifying; the sandbox key stays at rest. Preservation,
most of the year, is the discipline of *not touching*.

That is why Amber's activity cannot be a daemon, a scheduler, or a dock-event handler — the
recorded rulings close those doors by name. The docked drive is a precondition, never a
trigger; the shell may notice bare metal present and quietly offer, and it acts only when
asked. What remains, once every automatic path is refused, is exactly one honest shape for
activity: the season. Brief. Deliberate. Opened by a hand. Closed whole.

## The Anatomy of a Season

**The opening.** A season opens on Kaeden's explicit command from the calm shell, with the
physical precondition already met by his own hands. Nothing opens it for him — no calendar, no
udev whisper, no helpful process that waited. Sovereignty rests in the hand, and the opening
itself is worth a fact on the log.

**The work.** One season carries one honest job, bounded before it begins — a seal, a scrub, a
restore. The budget is named at the opening the way a garden names its bytes: which directory,
which drive, which archives the walk will cover.

**The warm seconds.** Heat is the season's most carefully rationed resource. In a seal, the
digest is computed and the record composed cold; the sandbox key warms only for the seconds of
signing, and returns cold before the drive is sleeved. In a scrub, the entire walk runs cold —
recomputing digests and checking signatures back to the root needs no signing authority at all —
and one brief unlock signs the reviewed batch of verification records at the end. The drive's
own passphrase warms the vault for reading and no longer. Warm for seconds, never for the
session: the rule is already law, and the season is the container that makes it checkable.

**The close.** The drive powers down, returns to its sleeve, returns to its shelf — or to the
bag that travels offsite. What the season leaves behind is only appended facts: an archive
record, its write-time verification, the scrub's fresh attestations, a correction where
something answered wrong. Both copies of the log carry them — the one riding the drive, so the
drive stays self-describing across the years, and the mirror riding the working tree, so the
catalog answers without a docking. Everything warm has been returned; nothing lingers. The
season closes the way a garden closes, and the archive's entire history is thereafter a pure
fold over what its seasons left behind.

## The Kinds of Season

| Season | Heat it permits | What it leaves on the log |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Seal** | Sandbox key warm for the seconds of signing, after the cold compose | An archive record, and its write-time verification record beside it |
| **Scrub** | Cold throughout the walk; one brief unlock signs the reviewed batch | A fresh verification record per sound archive — or a loud failed one, with a correction naming the wound |
| **Restore** | The drive passphrase warms to read; the signing key stays cold | The recovering hand's answer, checked bit-faithful against the sealed record |

Two more readings ride on the same word without needing rows of their own. A **drive's first
season** is its probation — the spec already says a new drive earns trust across its first
season of clean verifications, and an aging one rotates gently into low-stakes duty rather than
holding the last copy of anything. And the unwelcome kin belongs here too: a season may close by
*declining* — a refusal recorded as its own signed fact, the no given its receipt, in the same
shape the resin-refusal design already drew.

## Cadence — Named, Never Automatic

The spec names the rhythm plainly: a steady season, and quarterly serves. The word to hold onto
is *rhythm*. The calendar may invite; the catalog makes staleness visible, because an archive
whose newest verification record is aging is a question anyone can read off the fold; the shell
may notice a docked drive and offer. Only the hand opens. This is the CIVIC shape wearing
custody clothes: the outcome we actually want is *fresh verification facts on every archive*,
so the system measures exactly that and surfaces its absence — and never crosses from measuring
into acting. A scrub season overdue is a visible truth, not a trigger.

## What Lap 1's Season Bought, and Which Seasons Wait

Last night's season — opened by Kaeden's word after the three doors — carried the first lap
whole at fixture scale: one deterministic tree sealed to content-addressed resins and a
manifest, the manifest's digest pinned against an oracle from outside the family, the restore
compared bit-faithful, and the tampered resin refused loudly. One complete seal-verify-restore
turn, the smallest season that could honestly close. That is the working seed.

The seasons that wait are already named in the spec's own honest closing, and each arrives as
its own lap proven inside its own season: the live bare-drive seal under the paper-passphrase
custody; the encrypted vault at rest; the signing moved whole across the Kumara seam on the day
identity lives natively in the family; the Mantra log-slice seal that begins the
Amber-compatibility contract; the recovery rehearsal — a restore from a cold drive and a
recovery from a revoked sandbox key, each a witnessed season rather than a hoped-for one; and
the standing quarterly scrub, once real drives hold real archives worth walking. Nothing here
promises those tonight. The season is the vessel they will each arrive in.

## Why the Word Earns Its Keep

Season binds time the way Tally binds memory and named budgets bind the wire — one TAME
instinct, aimed at custody. It keeps Amber cold by default, because activity is the exception a
season contains. It keeps Kaeden sovereign, because every opening is his word and his hands. And
it keeps the history foldable, because a season's only permanent trace is appended, signed
facts. **Season** already sits in [`../context/LEXICON.md`](../context/LEXICON.md) beside gate, itinerary, lap, and green — the candidate earned its row; this note no longer parks that ask.
