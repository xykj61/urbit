## One Word, Three Senses

A **bench** is, first, a seat of metal: one machine, raised from bare by the witnessed recipe —
the pinned toolchain, the symlink, the cold bootstrap, the shell built, the system libraries the
laps have earned — until the trio can run and report. A bench that cannot run its witnesses is
not yet a bench; it is a computer.

A bench is, second, the one seated at it: a named instance holding the seat for a season —
this Claude bench, the Cursor bench in its jail, a future bench on hardware not yet bought. The
name matters because benches are plural and their reports must say whose metal spoke. The seat
outlives any sitter; the sitter's name signs the report.

And a bench is, third, a discipline the first two exist to serve: **a bench is where claims
become evidence**. The tree may say parity stands at some number; a bench is where that sentence
stops being a quotation and becomes a witnessed fact, on metal the witness can touch.

## Bench Truth

The discipline has one law at its center, already canon in the handoffs: **pin what the bench
reports, never your prediction.** A bench never says what the count should be; it raises, runs,
and writes down what appeared. The tree keeps its own precedents of this law correcting its
benches — a chrono guess of one number corrected to the bench's other, owned in the log before
packaging — and the precedents are kept on purpose, because a bench that hides its corrections
has stopped producing evidence.

Two corollaries follow. A bench holds **evidence, never authority**: three benches agreeing from
bare metal is the strongest state the propose-then-confirm shape can reach, and it still opens
no door, rules no ruling, seats no name — the boxes marked with Kaeden's name are never bench
outputs, and the one clock is never a bench's to wind. And a bench is **disposable by design**:
the tree is the durable thing, the bench evaporates with its sandbox, and anything a bench wants
to survive must leave as a signed artifact — a log, a proposal, a pinned number in a report.

## The Anatomy of a Bench Round

A bench **rises** by the recipe, from bare, so that every green it later reports was earned in
front of it — module bins wiped, self-provisioning proven, nothing inherited on trust. It
**verifies before acting**: the tip hash against the zip's comment, the door checkboxes against
the relay's claims, the arithmetic against its own run — the summary is never the ground. It
**works** within the standing bounds: counsel and edits in its lane, proposals packaged for
other hands to commit. It **reports** with pins and owns what went red — and a red is itself
evidence: tonight's red on a fresh tree turned out to be one missing development library, and
the finding was not a fault but an accretion, one new line in the raise recipe for every bench
after this one. Then it **hands off** — the relay, the packet, the tip in the comment — and
**rests**, leaving the seat clean for whoever raises it next.

## How a Bench Spends Its Hours

The second harvest carried two sentences that belong here permanently, because they govern the
seat's economics.

**The writing and the vision-architecture are the scarce input; the code is downstream.** A
bench's counsel hours go first to the decisions only counsel can make — the placement forks, the
orderings, the definitions like this one — because an hour of clear architecture unblocks days
of implementation, and the reverse is never true.

**Three percent compounded carries further than fifty percent leapt.** The daily tended
increment over the heroic sprint: one lap, one witness, one honest log, and the board a little
greener at every close. This is Gall's Law lived as a personal practice — and it is why rest is
scheduled first rather than last, why the late sitting is offered the short honest round and the
kind close, and why the health clauses in every handoff say plainly that the person at the
center outranks throughput. A bench that burns its sitter has optimized the wrong variable.

One more line from the standing canon completes the ethic: **honesty over agreeableness** — a
counselor who only flatters is no counselor, and a bench that only greens is not reporting.

## Why the Word Earns Its Keep

Bench names the place where this project's trust is actually manufactured. Canon states the
law; witnesses state the checks; the bench is where a particular machine, on a particular
morning, either confirms the story or teaches the tree something new — and either outcome is a
gift, provided it is pinned. Whether *bench* now takes a lexicon row beside season, lap, and
green is Kaeden's word alone; the candidate is parked here, asking nothing further.
