Identity, address, and key become one thing, and the tree of a life grows from it: federated with others, and ruled by none.*

## The Principle

One root, many keys. A person holds a single root — an owner key, the same one Aurora already trusts to wake a machine. From it, deterministically, every key they need is grown: a distinct key for each purpose the wider world asks of them, separated so a leak of one never touches another, unified so a single root is the only thing to keep and the only thing to recover.

Unity comes from *deriving*, never from *reusing*. One key serving many purposes would link what should stay apart and stake each against all; so instead the root derives a separate key for each purpose. The shared root gives the unity; the separation gives the safety. One backup, one recovery, many keys.

## Identity Is Address Is Key

A presence on the network needs no registry kept apart from its keys. The identity *is* the address *is* the key: to name a person is to name where they are reached and how they are verified, all at once. This folds three problems into one and removes the seams between them — the seams where confusion and forgery like to live.

## The Tree of a Life

From the root, a bounded tree of sub-identities derives, and its shape can mirror how a life actually branches: a few held close — the circle one acts and solves with — and the many those few carry forward, the works and projects and people in their care. Each sub-identity derives its own keys; each can be handed out and withdrawn; and the whole tree recovers from the one root, because every branch grows from it by a known path.

The shape is a gift of meaning, never a cage. The numbers that give it form are ours to choose for their resonance, yet the safety beneath rests entirely on the keys and never on the shape. We keep the meaning and the mechanism named apart — so the one stays beautiful and the other stays sound.

## Coordinated, Never Controlled

Roots gather into namespaces, and a namespace asks for a coordinator — one who registers, convenes, and bridges between namespaces. That coordinator must never become an owner. The keys stay in their holders' hands; the coordinator maps the namespace and brokers the alliances, yet can neither seize an identity nor silence one. A registrar, never a throne. The moment a coordinator could revoke a person, we would have rebuilt the very thing self-sovereignty exists to refuse.

## How It Sits With the Rest

The root is Aurora's owner key, grown into a life. Comlink carries these identities and the messages between them; Mantra names what each one says and signs; the gates keep each derivation honest. And because the root is one, the care we already practice — the cards today, and in time a split of the secret across trusted hands — becomes the keeping of a single thing rather than a scattered handful.

---

*May one well-kept root hold a whole life's presence. May the few held close and the many carried forward each find their place in the tree. May the keys stay in the hands that made them, helped by coordinators who never command — owned by the person, and ruled by no one.*
