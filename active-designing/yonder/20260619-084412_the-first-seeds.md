## Mission

To plant three parallel threads so the family does not outrun its ground: carry Comlink across a real device, grow the first running cells of Caravan and Tally, and read closely the packet and relay sources we cloned before we invent formats the wire will regret.

## Problem

Tally, Caravan, Brix, Tablecloth, Mantra, and Pond live richly in prose — `987_pond_foundation`, `997_recommended_architecture`, the caravan study — yet much of the near horizon (Comlink, Pond, the messenger) **leans on** supervision and bounded memory that do not yet run. If the device-wire races ahead alone, design drifts again: we would script a network atop abstractions we have not yet touched with code.

## Opportunity

Horizon 0 proved the method: primitive by primitive, gate by gate, until building **caught** design and passed it. The same method applies here. Caravan's full tree and Tally's full garden allocator are Horizon 2 compositions; their **seeds** are Horizon 1 work — small enough to love, large enough to compose.

## Architecture — Three Parallel Threads

### 1. Device wire (Comlink's next rung)

Emulated `virtio-net` between two QEMU instances; a sealed datagram crosses a true link. Unchanged in ambition; now explicitly **one of three** near threads, not the only one.

### 2. Caravan seed — watch one child, restart on fall

| Piece | Scope |
|-------|--------|
| Parent | Never exits; owns the supervision loop |
| Child | One process; may exit to simulate failure |
| Behavior | On non-zero exit, parent restarts child |
| Proof | Hosted test: child falls N times, parent brings it back; assert counts |

No kernel, no chain-loading yet. The seed is **supervision as a value and a loop** — the same discipline s6 teaches, expressed first in hosted Rye where the gates can reach it.

### 3. Tally seed — one region, asserted edges

| Piece | Scope |
|-------|--------|
| Region | Start, length, end fixed at creation |
| Allocate | Bump within region; overflow fails with reason |
| Release | Clear whole — one gesture, season ends |
| Proof | Tests: allocate to edge, assert failure past edge, clear and reuse |

Rye already clears a process garden on exit; this seed **names the edges** and makes them assertable — the allocator Pond's `mem_mb` will eventually measure.

### 4. Close reading — how others did packets and relays

Before Comlink's format hardens, read what we vendored:

- **Packet shape and naming** — continuity, content-addressing, hop semantics
- **Commit and finality** — quorum rules, what "done" means for a message
- **Relay behavior** — store-and-forward, subscription, spam resistance without a central filter

Each reading becomes a short note with **actionable consequences** for our modules — abstracted in active-designing where the clean room requires it, cited in `external-research` where gratitude belongs.

## Caveats

- **Parallelism is discipline, not dilution.** Three threads means each stays **small**; we do not open three large projects.
- **Seeds are hosted first.** Freestanding Caravan/Tally on RISC-V follows Aurora's relay pattern — seed on the host, then bare metal when the hosted proof is green.
- **Pond waits on the seeds.** `pond.rish` is already a policy value; opening it for real wants Caravan's isolation surface and Tally's bound — which these seeds begin.

## Longer Horizon

Caravan v1 composes the seed into a tree and chain-loaded startup. Tally v1 composes regions into named gardens per service. Pond v1 composes Caravan + Tally + policy. Comlink v1 composes the device wire + close-reading-informed packet format + unified keys. The seeds are not sidelines; they are the **floor** those compositions stand on.

## Conclusion

The property we earned — building level with design — is precious and perishable. Device-wire alone cannot hold it if the supervisor and the garden remain only names. Plant the smallest Caravan loop, the smallest Tally region, and the honest close readings **now**, in parallel with the wire, so every next layer grows from something that already runs.
