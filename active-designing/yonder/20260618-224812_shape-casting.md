## The Shape-Cast

Raw bytes arrive from the world — across the network, off a disk, out of another program — and the oldest mistake is to read them as the thing we hoped they were. A **shape-cast** refuses that mistake. It casts a run of bytes to a declared Rye type, asserts the invariants that type requires, and trusts the value only once its shape is proven. When the bytes already match, there is nothing to copy: the value is read where it lands. When they do not, the fault stops loudly, at the door, the instant a malformed thing tries to enter.

As pure validation this is wholly safe and strictly good — it designs away a whole class of fault: the confusion of one shape for another, the trusting of bytes that were never what they claimed. It is type-safety carried all the way out to the untyped edge of the system.

## The Bright Line — Shape Is Not Sanction

Here is the line that must never blur. Proving a thing's **shape** is not authorizing its **action**. A shape-cast can prove that raw bytes are well-formed — even that they are well-formed RISC-V instructions for the machine — and that tells us *what we hold*, never that it is *safe to set running*. "Well-formed" and "safe to act" are different claims, and the shape-cast makes only the first.

To treat a proven shape as a permission to run would be the one real danger here, so we refuse it by design. The shape-cast is the prerequisite to safe handling, never the grant of it.

## Two Layers of Safety

So safety stands in two layers, and the whole stance rests on holding them apart.

**Prevent by construction.** The shape-cast, the assertions, the sized types, and — as Rye grows — the compile-time proof that a value has one writer or many readers and never both at once, and that no reference outlives the garden it lives in: these make whole classes of fault simply unwriteable, before a program ever runs. Correctness designed in, paid for in nothing once it ships.

**Contain what remains.** What prevention cannot reach — the action of code once it is allowed to run, the world misbehaving, the fault no type could foresee — is held at runtime. **Pond** gives a bounded enclosure; **Caravan** isolates and supervises; **Tally** bounds the memory. So even a well-formed yet ill-meaning thing runs only inside a garden it cannot escape.

Prevent what we can, contain what we cannot. The first layer is the type system and the gates; the second is the enclosure and the kernel. Together they are correctness by construction with a safety net beneath — and the bright line between them, drawn and kept, is what makes both honest.

*May we prove what a thing is before we trust it, and never mistake its shape for our consent to its action. May the faults we can foresee be made unwriteable, and the rest be held in a garden they cannot leave.*
