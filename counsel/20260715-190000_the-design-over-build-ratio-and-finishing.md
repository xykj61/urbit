## The One Honest Finding

We generate design far faster than we ship running code, and this session made that plain. In a single day we seated Sala, prioritized Pool, and wrote studies on the fleet, mold nesting, non-vane strata, Zero-to-One, Kali Yuga, Sala-broadcast, and the Glowphone — nearly all of it proposal. The genuinely green, witnessed code was the Neth ladder, the toolchain raise, and (landed the same turn as this counsel) Sala B0. Real, and a small fraction of the volume.

The tree's own "designed-not-built" discipline marks this honestly, so nothing is hidden. Yet the *ratio itself* is the blind spot: naming and scoping is fast, satisfying, and feels like progress; building is slow and humbling. A cathedral of specs can quietly outpace the thing that runs. I own my part — I have been an eager amplifier of the design half, and a truer collaborator pushes back toward "ship one small real thing" more than I have.

**The growth edge, named in one line: finish and ship one lap to one real person before naming the next idea.** Sala B0 landing green in the same turn as this counsel is the first small proof that the edge can be walked, not only described.

## The Blind Spots, and Their Answers

1. **Design-over-build ratio.** Answer: no new vane, name, or study until the last one has a green witness or is explicitly parked. Open Pool P0 next; do not name Sala's successor first.
2. **Bus-factor and the vocabulary tax.** The system is legible mostly to its author and to an agent that reads the whole tree each session. Answer: the legibility work Quin already carries, and — for any real Glowphone or network — a real human collaborator who is not Keaton.
3. **Hardware realism.** Building a phone is a different universe from writing Rye. Answer: reflash-and-assemble from existing open hardware first (the two-body strategy already holds this), never custom silicon as the opening move.
4. **No real user has closed the loop.** Everything is built for hypothetical people. Answer: get one living person using one real thing — even a Sala broadcast of Keaton's own session.
5. **Breadth versus depth.** A magnificent worldview risks that nothing reaches shipping depth. Answer: drive the one flagship (Linengrow / Open Asks under Pool) all the way to a user.
