## The Instinct Was Right, and Here Is Why

`ORGANIZING.md`'s own "One Test for Placement" names eleven things a piece of writing might be doing — governing, naming why the work matters, reading a sky, teaching, carrying Linengrow's vision, deciding our own design, studying an external work, expanding intent, tracking what's open, recording what a session settled, implementing. Read that list straight through and one activity is missing: *answering a question Kaeden asked, about a decision this project itself is about to make.* That is not "studying an external work" — nothing external is being studied when the question is whether MALA has graduated. It is not "deciding our own design" either — `active-designing/`'s own charter calls that stack "the clean room" where *we* decide; counsel is the bench's answer *before* Kaeden decides, not the decision itself. Every counsel document this bench has written this season reached for the closest-sounding drawer, `external-research/`, because no drawer was actually built for what it is. The fix is not a tidiness pass. It closes a real gap in the filing law.

## What Stays, and Why Each One Earns It

**The settlement-seat pair** — `20260706-002612_the-settlement-seat-under-three-lights.md` and `20260706-005512_settlement-seat-verification-and-verdict.md` — read like counsel on first glance, and this bench almost moved the second one before reading its own status line: *"research for understanding; the seat parks under stop-and-park because it touches custody and ledger ground."* `external-research/`'s own charter carries a deliberate, standing carve-out for exactly this — money, settlement, and policy questions stay in the softer *research-for-understanding, not advice* register on purpose, distinct from ordinary architecture counsel. Both stay. The distinction worth holding going forward: counsel decides *how our own code should work*; this carve-out decides *nothing*, by design, about where money or policy should go.

**The TigerBeetle alignment study** stays for a plainer reason — its defining content, the greater part of every section, is a close reading of `tidy.zig` and `TIGER_STYLE.md`, an external codebase studied on its own terms before any of it is adopted. It carries recommendations the way most research here eventually does; the adoption record is a consequence of the study, rather than the document's reason for existing.

**Everything else in the folder** — the numbered essay series, the Urbit and seL4 and Wayland and POSIX studies, the settlement and userland and archive surveys — was never in question. This bench read past every title to confirm each one is a genuine study of a world outside this project, exactly what the folder's own charter asks for.

**One cluster is named here without a verdict, on purpose.** The `20260704` run of vocabulary pieces — "Gates, Arms, and the Urbit Lineage," "The Checkable Room," "Claim," "Itinerary and the Crossing Manifest" — read as neither research nor counsel; they read as this project defining its own words, which sounds closer to `active-designing/`'s charter than either folder now under discussion. This bench raises it once and sets it down again — a second reorganization is not what was asked for tonight, and folding it into this one risks the exact breadth this tree's own graduation rule warns against elsewhere.

## The Link-Hygiene Rule, Taken From the Tree's Own Precedent

The prior `external-research` rename carried an "on apply" instruction worth reusing verbatim rather than re-deriving: *repo-wide link hygiene... leave historical narration honest.* This bench reads that as two different things, not one, and recommends holding them apart precisely: a **path** is plumbing — the sixteen old `external-research/…` strings become `counsel/…` everywhere they appear, in every file, dated or living, since a corrected pointer is hygiene, not revisionism. The **sentence** around that path is narration — what a session log said happened, what a counsel document said it found, what TASKS recorded as decided — and none of that prose changes, ever, on the same one-clock law that already protects every dated artifact in this tree. Fourteen of the sixteen files' inbound references sit almost entirely inside other dated documents, which this rule leaves untouched apart from the bare path; the two genuinely living indexes — `external-research/README.md`'s own table and whatever `counsel/README.md` becomes — are the only places whole entries move rather than merely repoint.

## Closing the Gap in `ORGANIZING.md` Itself

The proposal is incomplete if it fixes today's filing without fixing the test that caused it. One line, in the same form as its eleven neighbors, added to "The One Test for Placement":

> **Advising on a decision this project is about to make** → `counsel/` (Claude's analysis, ruling, proposal, or recommendation, awaiting Kaeden's word).

Placed beside "Deciding our own design → `active-designing/`" and "Studying an external work → `external-research/`," it names the missing activity in the same breath as its two nearest neighbors, so the next counsel document has a drawer built before it needs one.

## A Front Door for the New Folder

A `README.md` for `counsel/`, matching its two siblings' shape:

> **What This Folder Is.** This is where the bench answers what Kaeden asked. The `counsel/` folder holds analysis, rulings, proposals, and recommendations delivered in response to a specific question about this project's own pending decisions — never a study of an external work (that stays in `external-research/`), never a decision made and seated (that lives in `active-designing/` or wherever the decision's own home is once Kaeden's word lands). Every piece here proposes; none of them seats anything. Propose-never-seat is not a discipline counsel merely follows — it is the whole reason this folder exists.
>
> Each entry stays exactly as written the day it was filed. A later counsel document may revisit, refine, or overturn an earlier one — the ruling that MALA had not graduated stands beside the fold-gap it found, both permanent, neither edited to match what came after.
