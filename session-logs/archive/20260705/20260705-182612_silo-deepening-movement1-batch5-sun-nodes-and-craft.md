## Reasoning trace

The nine talks gather into a few natural homes. Three concern the Sun in a house — the twelfth, the seventh, and the systematic Phaladeepika round — and their deepest shared teaching is a method: derive a house from its place in the sky, set the planet beside it, read agreement and clash, then blend the placement with the whole chart. That method became a teaching study of its own (#42), which let the two placement studies (#43, #44) stay focused and carry the recurring lesson that no single placement rules a chart. The examples those talks drew from public figures were generalized to the patterns they share, per the Silo Technique, rather than named.

Two talks concern the lunar nodes across an axis — Taurus–Scorpio and Cancer–Capricorn — and each became a study (#45, #46), built on one shared account of the nodes and then the particular synergy and clash of its axis, closing on the same resolution: substance and form, inner and outer, complete each other rather than compete.

The remaining four talks are two pairs on rising signs, Leo and Virgo. Each pair holds two things: a reusable method — every sign has a bright and a shadowed face, and dignity decides which shows — and the sign's own specific nature. The reusable method became a teaching study (#47). The sign-specific natures wait, parked on the folder question below.
