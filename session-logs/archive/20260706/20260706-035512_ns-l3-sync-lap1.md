## The Invariants, Stated Before the Algorithm

Sync carries content between peers, so its invariants come first. Every resin arriving from a peer is verified against its named digest before anything is appended, so a tampered resin refuses the whole crossing and the destination is left exactly as it was. Revisions append and never rewrite, matching NS-L2's immutability. A bolt synced to a peer recalls identically to its source, because a resin proven by its digest is the same bytes on every peer — this is referential transparency carried across the wire rather than only held at home. Have-already resins, whose digest the destination already holds, cross as refs while their bytes stay home; need-resin resins cross as bytes once. Everything stays bounded by the catalog's fixed capacity.

## What Was Built

Following the grain that put logic before carriage — OA-L1's fold before OA-L2's delivery — NS-L3's first lap proves the sync semantics in-process, before any wire. `mantra/recall_lap1.rye` grows three things: `hasResin`, which answers whether a catalog already holds a digest; `verifyResin`, the crossing's integrity check; and `syncRevision`, which walks a source bolt revision in two passes — first verifying every resin's digest and refusing the whole crossing on any mismatch, then counting the have-already and need-resin lanes and appending each leaf, with `appendLeaf` keeping revisions immutable and ordered. The read stays `recall`, the unit stays `bolt`, the content stays `resin`, and the crossing act stays `fetch` — no new name was minted; the lap reuses seated vocabulary entirely.

The witness sets a source peer holding a bolt revision and a destination peer already holding one of its resins, then syncs. The welcome path proves the dedup — one resin had-already as a ref, one needed as bytes — and proves referential transparency across peers, recalling each synced leaf on both catalogs and asserting identical bytes. The unwelcome paths refuse: a tampered resin refuses the whole crossing with nothing appended, and re-syncing a revision already held is refused as immutable.
