## The Origin, Owned

Kaeden asked where "footgun" came from, and the answer arrived with unexpected grace during the TigerBeetle study: **TigerBeetle's own `tidy.zig` uses it** — its banned-string lists open with `// Library footguns:` and `// Language footguns:` at lines 325 and 329 of the very file TAME's enforcement derives from. When this bench wrote *"the `witness_stop_after` footgun"* into the hardening menu at `20260707.042512`, it was echoing that source without noticing, and Cursor carried the word faithfully into TASKS, session logs, and a ring's informal name. So the word is not a stray from nowhere — it is Tiger Style's own register, arriving alongside Tiger Style's own laws. The distinction this pass draws is the one Radiant has always drawn: **the letter of a borrowed law crosses; its tone does not.** TigerBeetle writes in a fine, blunt engineering colloquial. Radiant writes so a sentence lands warmly read aloud, and a firearm metaphor for accidental self-harm does not.

## Scope — What Migrates, What Stands

**Living surfaces migrate:** code comments, READMEs, specs, TASKS' standing lines, and this tree's canon documents. **Dated artifacts stand as written:** session logs, counsel memos, and cut records keep their words on the one-clock law — history is accreted, never rewritten; the `042512` menu keeps its "footgun" forever, and this spec is the correction beside it rather than an edit inside it. **Landed tool filenames stand:** `caravan_subscribe_poll_source_crash.sh` and its kin keep the names their parity stanzas already call — a rename there is plumbing churn purchasing nothing; the prose around them migrates, the paths do not, per accrete-never-break.

## What Was Actually Changed Tonight

Four living sites carried the strays, and all four migrated: `recall_batch_delivery.rye`'s guard comment (*Dead-peer* → *Silent-peer*, and *often enough to hang* → *often enough to wait without bound*), `caravan/bounded.rye`'s simulation comment (*crashes* → *falls*), `mantra/README.md`'s batch row (*dead-peer timeout* → *silent-peer timeout*), and TASKS' completed-arc line (*Footgun → source-crash …* → *Misuse-guard → source-fall …*). A word-boundary sweep confirmed nothing else in authored code or living docs carries the retired forms — one apparent "hang" cluster dissolved into the substring of *change*, a reminder that this pass is judgment applied by a reader, which is exactly why it lands as a documented law and a repeatable grep list rather than a hard lint: prose lives in Radiant's care, and a machine gate over living sentences would refuse more good writing than it caught.
