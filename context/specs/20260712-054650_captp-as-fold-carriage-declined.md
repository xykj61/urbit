## The ruling

**We decline seating CapTP (or any live session / promise-pipelining idiom) as the carriage of the fold.**

The fold’s truth stays an append-only log of signed facts. Comlink’s sealed datagram — shared memory, hosted UDP, virtio — remains the way facts cross between our own ships. A session idiom may teach doors at the consumer edge; it does not become the room.

## Why (two reasons, both load-bearing)

1. **Principle.** CapTP is a *session* idiom (live references, promises, pipelining). The fold is a *record* idiom (signed facts, append-only, replayed). Seating one as the other tangles two value models that must stay composed. The wire serves the fold; it does not replace it.
2. **Accrete-never-break.** Capability-protocol drafts and implementations have moved version meaning under living apps. A wire whose semantics break between releases cannot be seated as the fold’s forever carriage.
