# Reviving Replaces Renaming

**Language:** EN
**Version:** `20260714.004428` (Pacific)
**Style:** Radiant (see `../RADIANT_STYLE.md`)
**Status:** Living — seats "reviving" as a first-class project concept and supersedes the earlier "mass rename deferred" framing

---

## The Decision

Keaton proposed, and this seats: **Glow OS grows by reviving, not by renaming.** Instead of ever renaming an existing module to a Glow-direction name — which would churn hundreds of references and produce un-reviewable diffs — we **revive**: rewrite the thing's proven essence in Glow as a *new artifact*, born with its Glow name, grown beside the elder, superseding it only when proven. A new sibling directory, [`active-reviving/`](../../active-reviving/README.md), is the home for revivals in motion, beside `active-designing/`.

## What This Supersedes

The naming-decisions spec ([`20260714-002123_naming-decisions-and-role-nesting.md`](20260714-002123_naming-decisions-and-role-nesting.md)) and the umbrella spec ([`20260713-235455_keaton-name-and-glow-os-umbrella.md`](20260713-235455_keaton-name-and-glow-os-umbrella.md)) both spoke of an eventual "mass rename, cheapest-first, on Keaton's word" — for Rye OS to Glow OS, and for modules to their vane roles. **That framing is retired.** There is no mass rename in the future of this project. Where those specs say "rename deferred," read "revived when the module is next re-grown in Glow." The role map in the naming-decisions spec still stands exactly as written — our names in code, Urbit names as pitch roles — and reviving is simply *how* a module eventually comes to carry its Glow-direction name: by being reborn with it, not relabeled into it.

## Why Reviving Is Better Than Renaming

- **Reviews stay small and human-checkable** — Keaton's stated optimization goal. The elder never churns; the revival is a fresh, self-contained artifact reviewed on its own merits against the elder's own witnesses.
- **Nothing breaks** — accrete-never-break, exactly. The elder keeps its name, home, and green witnesses and keeps running until the revival is proven.
- **Pitches stay legible** — a revival can be pitched to galaxy holders as "our Glow-native Clay," born with that name, with no history of a disruptive rename behind it.
- **It is already how the project works** — Pond revived ai-jail and superseded it on word; Rye is a thin frontend grown beside Zig's std; Neth revives the retired WOV-TigerBeetle pin. Reviving names a pattern already proven here, and gives it a folder.

## The Relationship of the Three Ideas

- **The role map** (naming-decisions spec) says *what Urbit role each of our modules fills* — the pitch vocabulary, true today, at zero cost.
- **Reviving** (this spec + `active-reviving/`) is *the process* by which a module is eventually re-grown in Glow as a new artifact that may carry its Glow/role name from birth.
- **Maze** is *where a revival shelters* while it earns its canonical name and graduates.

Together: pitch in Urbit's roles now, revive into Glow over time one proven artifact at a time, shelter the in-progress ones in Maze — and never rename anything, ever.
