## The Secular Foundations, in One Breath

A content-creation computational data market is a place where a maker offers content, data, or a computation over them; a consumer obtains access under clear terms; a price is struck in MALA; and the trade settles through Neth with a verifiable receipt. The mechanism is transparent (every offer wears its terms, every sale its receipt); the individual is private (revealing only what they choose, through a proof they control); everyone owns their own side (a key, not a login). It requires no particular kind of maker or cause — it is the general mechanism, and Linengrow is one values-specific flagship built in it.

Nothing is built — Pool has little to host until Glow runs an agent. When it can, smallest-first:

## The Discipline It Keeps

- **Grows from what works** (Gall's Law): Pool invents no primitive — it composes MALA, Neth, Granary, Mandi, Open Asks, Comlink, Sigma, Kumara.
- **Safety before performance** (TAME): a trade's receipt and settlement are asserted before any market-throughput claim.
- **Two Rooms**: the market's mechanism is checkable; the participant's privacy is proof-bound; no "feels like a market" stated as fact.
- **Secular, generic voice**: the market is described for any Acme employee, any maker, any content — the specific values register stays with Linengrow, the flagship.
