## One Honesty Edit — Kaeden's Call (file-as-is is the default)

Adoption does **not** depend on this. File the document as authored unless Kaeden asks for the edit.

The signature feature — memory that forgets — overstates in one phrase. A device that boots and holds a root key must persist its operating system and that key; only your *working data* truly dissolves. The reframe keeps the poetry and closes the engineering hole, and it reads stronger for being true. Two precise changes:

**In §IV "What It Is Made Of":**

> *Find:* `There is no storage chip at all, by design.`
> *Replace:* `There is no mass store for your work, by design — the device keeps only what it must to be itself and to be yours: the operating system in modest read-only flash, and your root key in a small secure element. Your working data finds no place to rest and be mined; it lives in working memory and dissolves with the power.`

**In §III "Memory that forgets":**

> *Find:* `It carries no onboard store waiting in the dark to be mined,`
> *Replace:* `It carries no onboard store for your work waiting in the dark to be mined,`

*Recommended: apply both* — the pitch will meet technical readers, and the honest framing is also the more compelling one. Leave them unmade if Kaeden prefers the current register; placement and links hold either way.

A separate, larger strengthening — naming the display class and a few candidate parts so §IV's claims stand under questioning — waits on the parts research Kaeden may ask for; it is not part of this filing.
